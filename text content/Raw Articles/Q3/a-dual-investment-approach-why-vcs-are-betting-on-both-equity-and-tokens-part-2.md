# A dual investment approach: why VCs are betting on both equity and tokens (Part 2)

*By Jonny Fry | *

In Part 1 of, _“A dual investment approach: why VCs are betting on both equity and tokens_ ”, we saw how leading venture capital (VC) funds are increasingly adopting dual investment strategies, so combining equity stakes with token holdings in Web3 startups. In this dual investment approach, equity offers governance rights, board seats and long-term value via company growth or exit events, whilst tokens provide immediate liquidity, direct exposure to network activity and protocol governance. VCs must now navigate the intersection of institutional investing and crypto-native protocols, capturing upside whilst managing new layers of risk and alignment. Understanding and actively managing these risk categories becomes essential for successful dual investment strategies.

The regulatory landscape for dual investment strategies remains complex and evolving; different jurisdictions have adopted varying approaches to token regulation, creating a patchwork of compliance requirements that investors must navigate carefully. Some regulators have embraced innovation-friendly frameworks that provide clarity for token investments, whilst others have adopted more restrictive approaches that limit investment flexibility. Venture capital firms operating dual strategies must ensure their token vesting schedules; governance rights and distribution mechanisms comply with applicable securities laws. This often requires sophisticated legal structuring and ongoing compliance monitoring that can add significant operational overhead to investment processes although the emergence of[ advanced subscription agreements](https://a16z.com/2020/04/30/token-or-equity/) and other innovative legal structures has helped formalise dual investment approaches whilst maintaining regulatory compliance. These instruments allow investors to commit capital to projects whilst providing flexibility around the ultimate form of their investment returns, whether through equity, tokens or hybrid structures. And despite these challenges, dual investment strategies provide valuable strategic optionality that single-asset approaches cannot match.

When regulatory environments shift or market conditions change, investors with diversified exposure can adjust their positions more effectively than those concentrated in a single asset class. This optionality proves particularly valuable in the rapidly evolving crypto ecosystem, where technological developments and regulatory changes can fundamentally alter the value proposition of different investment vehicles. Investors who maintain both equity and token positions can capitalise on whichever asset class performs better under changing circumstances whilst maintaining downside protection through diversification. The flexibility inherent in dual strategies also enables more sophisticated portfolio management techniques. Investors can use token positions for short-term liquidity needs whilst maintaining long-term equity positions for sustained value creation. They can also leverage staking opportunities and other token-specific yield generation mechanisms to enhance overall portfolio returns. As the Web3 ecosystem continues to mature, dual investment strategies are likely to become increasingly mainstream among institutional investors. The approach offers a compelling framework for engaging with tokenised economies whilst maintaining the governance rights and downside protection associated with traditional equity investments. The evolution of this investment model reflects broader changes in how venture capital firms conceptualise their role in the ecosystem. Rather than simply providing capital and strategic guidance to individual companies, VCs are increasingly positioning themselves as partners in network development and protocol governance.

This expanded mandate requires more sophisticated investment approaches that can capture value across multiple dimensions of network growth and development, and the dual approach also aligns with the fundamental philosophy of decentralisation that underpins many Web3 projects. By holding both equity and tokens, investors demonstrate commitment to both company success and broader network health, signalling long-term alignment with protocol communities and stakeholders. The dual investment model of combining equity and token exposure represents a sophisticated evolution in venture capital strategy that reflects the unique characteristics of Web3 investments. Firms [such as Polychain Capital and DCG](https://www.nftgators.com/polychain-capital-co-leads-15m-funding-for-ip-tokenization-platform-aria/) have launched stage-specific funds - some purely for token allocations, others for later-stage equity rounds. This separates early-stage risk from scalable bets that target Layer 2 protocols or modular blockchain infrastructure whilst Binance Labs’ incubator, for example, adapts to projects’ evolving needs with flexible investment vehicles. So, although this approach introduces additional complexity and risk management challenges, it also provides access to multiple value creation engines that can drive superior returns in successful projects - balancing this dual investment style demands new skill in risk mitigation. Tokens may be subject to sudden market drops but sophisticated funds stress transparent disclosure, algorithmic portfolio rebalancing and insurance or custodial safeguards. Moreover, governance can be a double-edged sword - token holders may push for aggressive growth or high fee distribution whilst equity holders prioritise stability. Success hinges on aligning incentives and fostering network health, with VCs playing an active role in voting, protocol upgrades and community-building. VCs are no longer passive financiers but active stewards of protocol development and corporate strategy. And this approach unlocks multidimensional value creation, capturing board-level influence, direct protocol participation and fluid market exits. The complexities of custody, compliance and coordination are real, but funds that master dual strategies enjoy risk-adjusted returns and strategic agility.

As regulatory frameworks continue to evolve and market infrastructure matures, the tools and processes for managing dual investment strategies will likely become more refined and accessible. This evolution should make the approach increasingly attractive to a broader range of institutional investors seeking exposure to the growing Web3 ecosystem. Interestingly, the firms that have pioneered dual investment strategies, including Paradigm and a16z, have demonstrated the potential for this approach to generate exceptional returns whilst maintaining appropriate risk management. Certainly, their success provides a roadmap for other investors seeking to participate effectively in the tokenised economy of the future. Ultimately, the dual investment model reflects the hybrid nature of Web3 ventures themselves, which combine traditional business operations with novel tokenised network effects. As these ecosystems continue to grow and mature, investors who master the complexities of dual exposure strategies will be best positioned to capture the full spectrum of value creation opportunities that emerge from this technological revolution.
