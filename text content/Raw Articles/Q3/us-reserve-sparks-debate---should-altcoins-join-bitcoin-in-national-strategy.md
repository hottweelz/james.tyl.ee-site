# US reserve sparks debate - should altcoins join Bitcoin in national strategy?

*By Jonny Fry | *

As President Donald Trump mandates the creation of a crypto reserve, the US finds itself at a pivotal moment in the evolution of digital finance. Whilst Bitcoin (BTC) is widely expected to form the backbone of this reserve, a fierce debate has emerged over whether altcoins, particularly Ripple’s XRP, should be included. This discourse intertwines issues of economic strategy, decentralisation philosophy, regulatory compliance and geopolitical positioning, all whilst global markets watch closely. At the forefront of this push is [Senator Cynthia Lummis,](https://cryptopotato.com/senator-lummis-proposes-67b-strategic-bitcoin-reserve-to-back-usd/) who presented a bold proposal at the Bitcoin 2024 Conference in Nashville to create a US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve aimed at bolstering the dollar’s global dominance and long-term economic resilience. Lummis envisions a government-operated, decentralised network of secure Bitcoin vaults managed by the Treasury, acquiring one million Bitcoin units over time, approximately 5% of the total BTC supply - the equivalent scale of US gold reserves. This acquisition would amount to about $67 billion at current market prices.

President Trump publicly endorsed similar sentiments at the conference, suggesting his administration would retain [100% of Bitcoin holdings for the national reserve](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/03/02/trump-announces-strategic-crypto-reserve-including-bitcoin-solana-xrp-and-more.html) purposes. Meanwhile, Robert F. Kennedy Jr., an independent presidential candidate, has proposed an even more aggressive strategy to acquire four million BTC, mirroring the gold reserve proportionally. The strategic Bitcoin reserve concept is modelled after established commodities stockpiles, acting as a financial buffer to mitigate economic shocks during crises. A Reuters report has detailed that the government currently holds approximately [200,000 BTC from federal seizures, worth over $21 billion](https://www.reuters.com/technology/how-would-us-bitcoin-strategic-reserve-work-2025-01-24/), but it is unclear whether acquisitions beyond confiscated assets would be part of the plan or how storage and security logistics would be managed. Bitcoin maximalists champion BTC exclusively due to its robust decentralisation, fixed supply and growing institutional adoption and, as Senator Lummis emphasised, Bitcoin embodies “digital gold” and offers a hedge against inflation and currency devaluation - critical in the face of mounting US national debt exceeding $36 trillion. However, scepticism remains. Critics, including [BitMEX’s co-founder, Arthur Hayes](https://www.ccn.com/news/crypto/us-bitcoin-reserve-backfire-crypto-arthur-hayes/#:~:text=Arthur%20Hayes%20warns%20against%20the,future%20BTC%20sales%20by%20governments.), warn of political motives behind reserve plans, fearing future administrations might liquidate the Bitcoin stockpile for short-term fiscal relief whereby undermining the asset's strategic purpose. Furthermore, the [European Central Bank (ECB) has rejected Bitcoin as a reserve asset](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/ecb-lagarde-bitcoin-reserve/) due to its price volatility, liquidity concerns and susceptibility to illicit finance. ECB President Christine Lagarde underscored its lack of sufficient “ _liquidity, security and safety”,_ posing significant barriers for adoption among federal agencies. Contrasting Bitcoin maximalism is [Ripple’s CEO, Brad Garlinghouse,](https://www.ccn.com/news/crypto/ripple-ceo-backs-us-crypto-shift-strategic-reserve/) who, not surprisingly, advocates for a diversified digital asset reserve that encompasses a spectrum of cryptocurrencies beyond Bitcoin. Garlinghouse dismisses maximalism as “ _the biggest obstacle to crypto’s evolution_.” However, this stance faces strong resistance from Bitcoin purists such as [Strike’s CEO, Jack Mallers](https://www.coinspeaker.com/xrp-vs-bitcoin-crypto-leaders-debate-over-us-reserves/), and Riot Blockchain VP, Pierre Rochard, who underline Bitcoin’s stringent decentralisation and argue that XRP’s significant holdings under Ripple Labs compromise its suitability for a national reserve. Critics further contend that XRP’s lobbying efforts aim to secure favourable positioning, so challenging Bitcoin’s primacy. Thus, the inclusion of XRP or other altcoins transforms the debate from a mere financial one into a complex strategic and ideological battle shaping crypto’s evolution.

Furthermore, the regulatory landscape is pivotal. The US government’s move towards a strategic digital asset reserve arrives amidst ongoing policy ambiguity. The recent creation of a Senate crypto subcommittee, led by Senator Lummis, emphasises pro-crypto regulatory reforms aimed at fostering innovation whilst establishing consumer protections. Meanwhile, states such as Wyoming, Utah and North Dakota are piloting [local Bitcoin reserves](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/16-states-betting-u-bitcoin-100000220.html?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAKiwSUrS40ebp2U0lxpCtl1qmcOdXqbqcXkT-2i8vTaf9iurSuR9SgDBsMjfYPEWgA-TjShodnuzQSMUZu4l-4Hyw8osyG-QOTNibsGAf1vW89XspY5daKZBtB2DVBjcNB0acVCTU8vknifPh4xJR7PwWMZ-XnUXzBauwrqH7az-), experimenting with frameworks that could inform a federal approach. At federal level, the GENIUS Act and other legislative efforts seek to clarify stablecoin and crypto regulations, framing stablecoins as essential components of digital finance but also as subjects of stringent AML and compliance oversight. On the international front, countries are adopting divergent approaches - Brazil’s RESBit proposal contemplates allocating [5% of national reserves to Bitcoin](https://www.bitdegree.org/crypto/news/15-billion-bitcoin-bet-brazil-considers-adding-btc-to-national-reserves) to hedge against currency fluctuations; [Russia explores Bitcoin’s utility in evading Western sanctions](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/uk-targets-sanctions-circumvention-and-crypto-networks-exploited-by-russia); whilst [Japan explicitly rejects Bitcoin for its public reserves](https://cryptodnes.bg/en/japan-rejects-bitcoin-for-national-reserves-while-private-sector-continues-to-invest/), favouring traditional foreign currency and bond holdings for stability.

Establishing a US strategic Bitcoin reserve would have profound domestic and global repercussions. Indeed, creating a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve [“](https://www.onesafe.io/blog/future-of-crypto-payroll-lessons-from-us-treasury) _[could change everything for crypto payroll. This initiative suggests a more serious interest in digital assets and could lead to clearer rules that help more companies pay folks with crypto](https://www.onesafe.io/blog/future-of-crypto-payroll-lessons-from-us-treasury)_[.”](https://www.onesafe.io/blog/future-of-crypto-payroll-lessons-from-us-treasury) Economically, it could legitimise Bitcoin as a reserve asset akin to gold, potentially enhancing market confidence and stabilising the dollar’s long-term dominance. This strategic positioning could influence global crypto adoption patterns, encouraging allied states to follow suit and accelerating the integration of digital assets in sovereign finance. However, critics warn of Bitcoin’s infamous price volatility exposing the financial system to sharp fluctuations. Whilst Bitcoin’s historical trajectory shows long-term growth, short-term dips could destabilise markets if reserves represent a large asset share. In addition, this risk has fuelled calls for diversification into stablecoins and other digital assets that may offer smoother valuations and broader utility. And technological trends compound this debate - the rise of AI-driven crypto assets, tokenised dollar transactions and programmable money suggests strategic digital reserves will need flexible asset compositions to adapt to rapidly evolving financial technologies.

So, the US stands at a decisive crossroads as it charts the future of digital currency strategy. The formation of a national digital asset reserve encapsulates broader questions about economic security, technological innovation and financial sovereignty. Whether the US opts for a Bitcoin-exclusive reserve or embraces a diversified portfolio including altcoins such as XRP, the decision will reverberate across geopolitics, markets and the trajectory of global crypto adoption. As the presidential working group continues evaluation, stakeholders from policymakers to investors await clarity that balances innovation, stability and regulatory prudence. Undoubtedly, this moment offers the potential to redefine the US’ financial landscape - and global leadership in the 21st-century digital economy.
