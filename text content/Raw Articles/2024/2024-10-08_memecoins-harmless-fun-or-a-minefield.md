# Memecoins - harmless fun or a minefield of scams?

Memecoins have captured the imagination of millions of people globally on-line, whereby blending internet culture, humour and finance. These digital assets, often inspired by viral memes, represent more than just a fleeting joke. Take [Dogecoin](https://www.markets.com/education-centre/celebrity-influence-on-dogecoin/#:~:text=Elon%20Musk's%20role%20in%20promoting,phenomenon%20in%20its%20own%20right.), for example - what began as a humorous project rapidly evolved, attracting the attention of high-profile figures such as Elon Musk. However, this rise prompts an essential question: can something rooted in humour hold lasting value, or is it simply a bubble waiting to burst? The volatility of memecoins suggests that whilst they offer excitement, they also carry significant risks for those chasing quick profits. An extensive list and the latest market data can be viewed [here](https://www.forbes.com/digital-assets/categories/meme-coins/?sh=65fc16d8528a).

Memecoins often walk a fine line between being playful, financial expressions, and being risky, hype-driven investments. Unlike other digital assets, which may have specific use cases or underlying technology, many memecoins lack intrinsic value or utility. Their value is often propelled by the excitement of online communities and speculative investors looking to profit from short-term gains. This leads to meteoric rises, where prices skyrocket seemingly overnight, only to come crashing down as early investors cash out. Such volatility makes memecoins prime candidates for ‘[pump and dump’](https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/investing-basics/glossary/pump-and-dump-schemes) schemes, where prices are artificially inflated through coordinated buying. Once the hype reaches a peak, early investors sell off their holdings, leaving latecomers with steep losses. The ease with which new memecoins can be created has also given rise to ‘[rug pulls](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/pull-the-plug)’, where creators vanish with investors' funds after gaining traction. And in this chaotic landscape, memecoins challenge the notion of value in the digital age - i.e. are they a legitimate financial innovation born from internet culture or merely vehicles for exploitation? Their rise forces us to confront the risks of unchecked speculation and ask ourselves whether these coins truly represent a democratisation of finance or a new breed of mass deception.

Pump-and-dump tactics are a well-known problem in the memecoin space, taking advantage of the excitement and absence of real value in many of these tokens. These schemes rely on driving up the token’s price through organised buying, followed by a sharp decline as early investors cash out whereby leaving latecomers with heavy losses as mentioned before. The swift growth of platforms such as [Pump.fun](https://pump.fun/) highlights how susceptible memecoins are to these manipulative practices. Pump.fun, which debuted on [Solana](https://solana.com/), became the fastest-growing protocol in the crypto ecosystem, hitting [$100 million in revenue in just 217 days](https://www.cryptopolitan.com/solana-pumpfun-revenue-surpass-100m/). Unsurprisingly, its appeal to retail speculators and the high fees it charges have contributed to this success; this success stems from its user-friendly platform, which allows anyone to [create a memecoin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQS93hTmxkE) for as little as $2. Once a memecoin reaches a specific market cap, it can be listed on Raydium, a decentralised exchange on Solana. Notably, Pump.fun has launched nearly 2 million tokens, fuelling significant activity on the network and generating over [$100billion of revenue](https://www.theblock.co/data/decentralized-finance/dex-non-custodial/pump-fun-revenue-daily) for Pump.Fun. And what sets Pump.fun apart is its simplicity and accessibility, so making it a popular choice for memecoin creators. It charges a small fee for token creation and a 1% commission on transactions, with additional incentives such as rewarding successful token creators with [0.5 SOL](https://www.cryptopolitan.com/solana-pumpfun-revenue-surpass-100m/) (SOL being Solan’s token). However, despite its record growth, the platform's rise has also seen a sharp decline in activity for many of the memecoins created. As the platform facilitated the creation of over 500,000 tokens, many were launched with questionable intent, fuelling concerns about memecoins being used as vehicles for exploitation. Whilst its model has inspired competitors such as TRON’s, [SunPump](https://sunpump.meme/), Pump.Fun’s own rapid adoption underscores its influence in the crypto space, despite criticism of enabling fraudulent activities (e.g. rug-pulls and market manipulation). Significantly, this trend highlights the volatile and speculative nature of memecoins, hence investors need to tread carefully in such an unpredictable market. [Mitchell Demeter,](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mitchelldemeter/) CEO of Boost.fun, considers that: "_The memecoins space was founded on fun. Consumer behaviour has adopted these expectations and shown interest in the creative and exciting nature of memecoins. The space is ready for more competition from platforms, leaning into the exciting gamification of memecoin hunting, keeping it fun, keeping it open. Boost.fun is building a memecoin launchpad loaded with nuggets like jackpots and affiliate revenue sharing for all members of the community. The intention is to keep control in the hands of creators and the participants growing the memecoin space"._

Meanwhile, SunPump, a memecoin platform initially on Solana, has [recently shifted its focus to the TRON blockchain](https://beincrypto.com/sunpump-shifts-focus-from-solana/). This move comes as Solana’s Pump.fun experiences a significant decline in activity, with its lowest number of launches in 151 days. From an average of 15,217 daily launches between August 5 and 11, the number dropped to 8,003 by August 25 - a 47% decline. Revenue also fell nearly 40%, from an average of 5,880 SOL per day to 3,539 SOL. In contrast, SunPump, launched on [TRON by Justin Sun](https://www.thecoinrepublic.com/2024/09/16/justin-sun-reveals-6-things-to-watch-out-for-on-tron-this-week/), is seeing rapid growth. The platform has facilitated over 46,000 token launches since August 9, despite being 77% behind Pump.fun’s 193,000 launches in the same timeframe. Moreover, SunPump aims to expand market share by increasing the energy cap to 120 billion TRX, enhancing network activity and reducing transaction fees. This strategy could certainly make SunPump a strong competitor, potentially reshaping the memecoin landscape on TRON. And, whilst Pump.fun has garnered significant attention for its rapid growth, several other platforms [offer alternatives for memecoin enthusiasts](https://www.similarweb.com/website/pump.fun/competitors/). Among the top competitors is [Dexscreener.com](https://dexscreener.com/), renowned for its comprehensive real-time price charts and trading history across multiple blockchains such as Ethereum, Binance Smart Chain and Polygon. [Solscan.io](https://solscan.io/) provides a user-friendly scanning tool specifically for the Solana ecosystem, offering real-time updates on transactions and tokens.

Another notable competitor is [Jupiter (jup.ag)](https://jup.ag/), which excels as a swap aggregator on Solana, helping users find the best trade rates. [Dextools.io](https://www.dextools.io/app/en) and [Birdeye.so](https://birdeye.so/) also stand out, with Dextools offering detailed DeFi charts and token info, while Birdeye provides multi-chain crypto data tracking. [Raydium.io](https://raydium.io/) and [Magic Eden (magiceden.io)](https://magiceden.io/) further diversify options, with Raydium supporting DeFi evolution and Magic Eden leading in NFT marketplaces. These platforms showcase diverse approaches to token management and trading, providing valuable alternatives to Pump.fun’s model. Indeed, memecoins, often born from internet jokes, offer profound insights into the evolving landscape of finance and human behaviour in the digital age. Their ability to rapidly gain millions in value raises fundamental questions about our understanding of value creation. Questions to consider include: essentially, does the rise of these coins signify mere market irrationality, or does it highlight a shift where cultural influence and online communities can significantly impact financial outcomes?; as we witness the rise of these currencies fuelled by online communities, should we consider whether this represents the democratisation of financial markets or just another example of mass speculation?; how do we weigh the promise of fast profits against the potential for quick losses?; and, are memecoins really democratising finance or are they simply creating fresh opportunities for speculative bubbles and scams?

Certainly, the prevalence of scams and fraudulent schemes in the memecoin space further underscores the challenges of trust in a decentralised financial world. Traditional trust mechanisms are being tested, replaced by collective belief systems that drive market behaviour and, indeed, could memecoins offer lessons for financial markets as to how to engage with investors globally on-line. As we witness the rise and fall of these digital assets, the enduring lessons revolve around the nature of risk, value and community in the digital age. So, are we adapting to a new financial reality or are we simply repeating old mistakes with a modern twist? Without doubt though, these insights challenge us to reflect on how we engage with and understand value in the modern financial landscape.
