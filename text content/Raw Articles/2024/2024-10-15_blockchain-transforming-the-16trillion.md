# Blockchain transforming the $16+trillion repo market

The [repo market](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/what-is-the-repo-market-and-why-does-it-matter/), or repurchase agreement market, plays a crucial role in global finance by providing liquidity and facilitating short-term borrowing between financial institutions. Essentially, a repurchase agreement is a form of short-term, collateralised loan where one party sells securities to another with the promise of repurchasing them later at a higher price. Furthermore, this market enables institutions with excess cash to lend to those in need of liquidity, often using US Treasury securities as collateral. Yet, despite diminishing since 2007, the global repo market remains significant with an [outstanding size of over $16 trillion and a daily turnover of around $3 trillion](https://www.icmagroup.org/market-practice-and-regulatory-policy/repo-and-collateral-markets/icma-ercc-publications/frequently-asked-questions-on-repo/4-how-big-is-the-repo-market/). And with daily transactions volumes ranging between $2trillion and $4trillion, the repo market is a vital component for maintaining liquidity - being closely monitored by central banks such as the Federal Reserve.

This market is also [essential for maintaining liquidity and stability](https://www.icmagroup.org/market-practice-and-regulatory-policy/repo-and-collateral-markets/icma-ercc-publications/frequently-asked-questions-on-repo/3-what-is-the-role-of-repo-in-the-financial-markets/) within the financial system by offering short-term funding solutions to financial institutions. Through ‘repos’, securities are sold with a commitment to repurchase them later, allowing secured borrowing for entities requiring immediate capital. This mechanism is vital for institutional investors, central banks and securities dealers, enabling them to address liquidity needs whilst preserving their long-term investments. Moreover, the worldwide repo market is substantial. In mid-2016, it was worth approximately $8.8trillion globally according to the [Bank of International Settlements (BIS)](https://www.bis.org/publ/cgfs59.pdf) \- the Euro area had a market size of $2.8trillion, representing 32% of the global market and accounting for a similar percentage of outstanding government debt securities; the US market followed with $2.7trillion, making up 30% of the total market and covering 16% of government debt; and Japan's repo market, valued at $2.2trillion, represented 23% of the global total and 21% of the government debt securities. Correspondingly, the UK had a repo market size of $900billion, representing 10% of the global total and covering 33% of its government debt securities; Canada's repo market stood at $211billion, about 2% of the global total, whilst Australia's repo market reached $106billion, about 1% of the global total. Notably, Switzerland has a much smaller repo market, valued at $10billion, contributing just 0.1% of the global total and covering 11% of government debt securities in the jurisdiction.

In addition, blockchain technology offers transformative benefits to the repo market, essential for institutional financing. By [digitising workflows and automating settlements](https://www.omfif.org/focm_ch4_repo-market/), blockchain enhances efficiency, transparency and security in repo transactions. Furthermore, tokenisation allows for the seamless transfer and management of collateral, reducing counterparty risk significantly. Institutions can optimise collateral use, streamlining its delivery across various regions and jurisdictions. And this digital approach mitigates operational constraints that have historically hampered repo transactions, such as the fragmentation of collateral and lengthy settlement processes. Real-time settlement abilities also enable market participants to meet their funding needs more effectively with blockchain technology allowing for settlement timings to be customised for specific transactions, making intra-day repos possible when advantageous - this change certainly enhances liquidity management whilst reducing the chances of transaction failures, which boosts market stability. And, as the repo market continues its digital evolution, the opportunities for greater transparency and operational efficiency are becoming clear. Blockchain technology is already being used for the securities lending and repo markets by [streamlining operations and enhancing market efficiency](https://www.garp.org/risk-intelligence/market/securities-lending-repo-060223). As major players such as BlackRock highlight the potential of tokenisation to transform capital markets, firms are increasingly recognising the value of distributed ledger technology (DLT). Research indicates that the collateral and securities borrowing market, particularly the repo market, faces significant operational friction, making it ripe for blockchain implementation.

Meanwhile, platforms such as HQLAᵡ and JPMorgan’s, Onyx, are already processing billions in securities lending volumes, demonstrating the real-world benefits of blockchain. These systems facilitate instant and simultaneous transfers, reducing traditional custody chains and settlement cycles. By enabling delivery versus delivery (DvD) and delivery versus payment (DvP) transactions, they significantly cut down settlement times and increase liquidity. Ultimately, blockchain's integration into these markets not only enhances operational efficiency but also positions firms to optimise collateral management and reduce capital costs, paving the way for a more agile financial ecosystem. The examples below illustrate the transformative potential of blockchain in the repo market, streamlining processes, enhancing transparency and significantly reducing settlement times:

> · [JP Morgan took a pioneering step in 2020](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/payments/wallets/blockchain-onyx-asset-tokenization) by conducting intraday repo transactions using its Onyx blockchain platform. This novel solution allowed for the immediate exchange of cash and collateral, sharply reducing the typical T+2 settlement period to real-time. This increase in liquidity lowered counterparty risks and improved transparency, providing participants with a clear view of asset ownership throughout the process.
> 
> · [Broadridge Financial Solutions further advanced this trend](https://www.broadridge.com/article/capital-markets/dlr-transacts-1-trillion-a-month) by launching a distributed ledger platform for repo transactions in 2021. This system enabled multiple parties to trade repos in a fully automated environment, achieving instant settlement and automated collateral transfers. During its testing phase, the platform processed over $31 billion in average daily volume, significantly cutting down the time spent on administrative tasks such as collateral confirmation and contract negotiation whilst enhancing operational transparency.
> 
> · In collaboration with Deutsche Börse, [HQLAx developed a blockchain-based platform that revolutionises collateral management in the repo market](https://deutsche-boerse.com/resource/blob/1738322/387052f240a42bfdcb1b76071de00559/data/hqlax_how_distributed-_ledger_technology_can_benefit_the_securities_lending_market.pdf). This innovation [allows the transfer of ownership of securities](https://www.hqla-x.com/post/impact-of-distributed-ledger-technology-in-global-capital-markets) without the physical movement of underlying assets, optimising the use of high-quality liquid assets (HQLA) and facilitating immediate trade settlements (which reduces settlement risks). [Banco de España also joined the fray](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/bank-of-spain-unveils-wholesale-cbdc-trial-with-minsait/) by piloting a blockchain-based solution for cross-border repo transactions in collaboration with other European central banks. This initiative streamlined settlement processes across jurisdictions, demonstrating blockchain's potential to eliminate operational inefficiencies and enhance transparency in cross-border financial operations.
> 
> · The [Luxembourg Stock Exchange, in partnership with Clearstream, launched a blockchain initiative](https://www.clearstream.com/clearstream-en/newsroom/210621-2690322) focusing on the settlement of securities tokens, including those used in repo transactions. This initiative provided near-instantaneous settlements, lowering transaction costs whilst increasing security and auditability. [The European Investment Bank (EIB), in collaboration with Société Générale, issued €100 million in digital bonds](https://www.eib.org/en/stories/cryptocurrency-blockchain-bonds) on the Ethereum blockchain. Whilst primarily aimed at bonds, this project has significant implications for the repo market, so highlighting how digital bonds can serve as collateral as well as allowing for faster, more transparent settlements.
> 
> · Lastly, the [Swiss Digital Exchange](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/commerzbank-swiss-wholesale-cbdc-six-digital-exchange-sdx/) (SDX) has developed a [blockchain platform to trade tokenised securities](https://www.globalgovernmentfintech.com/swiss-national-bank-puts-cbdc-into-production-for-bond-transactions/), achieving successful [repo transaction tests](https://www.hkdca.com/swiss-digital-bonds-settled-with-wholesale-cbdc/) with near-instant settlements and improved transparency. Built in compliance with Swiss regulations, the platform reduces counterparty risk by settling cash and collateral in real time.

Essentially, when considering the examples shown above, blockchain technology is positioned to significantly change the repo market by optimising settlement processes, enhancing transparency and reducing counterparty risk. And, with the pace of adoption accelerating, financial institutions are expected to turn to blockchain for real-time settlements, automated collateral management and improved efficiency in cross-border transactions. The potential of smart contracts to automate intricate repo agreements could lead to decreased operational costs and, moreover, as regulators become more familiar with blockchain technology, broader acceptance in financial markets is expected. Hence, as blockchain-powered platforms drive this transformation, the key question posed, is: how long before the repo market is fully digitised?
