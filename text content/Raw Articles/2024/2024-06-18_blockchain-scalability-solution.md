# Blockchain scalability solution

The cryptocurrency sector confronts a critical scaling difficulty, particularly regarding transaction speed; transactions speeds on some blockchains are slower than standard payment methods. Nevertheless, according to data from CC Data, stablecoins in April alone witnessed [$2.18 trillion](https://ccdata.io/reports/stablecoins-cbdcs-report-april-2024) of turnover, compared to Visa which typically has a turnover of less than [50%](https://annualreport.visa.com/financials/default.aspx) of this volume. Yet, despite this, crypto groups seek novel ways to bypass this barrier. Continuous research and development have the potential to result in significant advancements, perhaps leading to lightning-fast transaction speeds in the future. [Public blockchains](https://www.investopedia.com/news/public-private-permissioned-blockchains-compared/) have scalability limits resulting in slow transaction speeds (restricted throughput), exorbitant transaction fees and excessive delays in transaction confirmation. For example, according to [Y-Charts](https://ycharts.com/indicators/bitcoin_average_transaction_fee), this time last year a Bitcoin transaction as $5 recently it has been as a high as $86 to execute a transaction To execute transactions on the Bitcoin blockchain is not only sometimes very expensive but can only be done [7 transactions a second](https://trustmachines.co/learn/bitcoin-scalability/#:~:text=A%20new%20Bitcoin%20block%20is,Transaction%20Cost.) (tps) - this is far lower than the current number of card payments globally, which is just below 23,000 tps. Furthermore, validating transactions on a blockchain might take an hour or more, making it a challenge for transactions that require instant confirmation. Hence, understanding blockchain’s scalability necessitates analysing the [Blockchain Scalability Trilemma](https://www.dbs.com.sg/personal/articles/nav/investing/what-is-the-blockchain-trilemma#:~:text=expensive%20to%20use.-,What%20is%20the%20Blockchain%20Trilemma%3F,security%2C%20and%20scalability%2C%20simultaneously.) which postulates that high degrees of _decentralization, scalability_ and _security_ cannot all be achieved simultaneously. Instead, blockchain systems should prioritise two of these three features - in other words, trade-offs among these components are unavoidable.

So, to compete with centralized systems, blockchain networks must prioritise scalability. The Blockchain Trilemma emphasises the difficulty of fulfilling all three criteria simultaneously so in order to address this trilemma, researchers and developers are looking for novel ways to improve scalability. The ultimate goal is to create a blockchain system that is entirely decentralized, safe and scalable and whilst the possibility of such a solution is still being investigated, achieving the best balance between these three factors is critical to the growth of blockchain technology. Researchers are investigating numerous ideas and ways to overcome blockchain networks' restricted speed and expensive expenses of certain blockchains; these ideas aim to increase the networks' ability to process transactions and overall capacity while maintaining the security and decentralization and include:

> · [layer 1 (on-chain) solutions](https://moralis.io/web3-wiki/top/layer-1-blockchains-l1s/#:~:text=Layer%2D1%20blockchains%2C%20known%20as,and%20are%20decentralized%20and%20immutable.)

Layer 1 solutions enhance the core functionalities of a blockchain network. They can increase the maximum block size or minimize the time required to validate blocks. Sharding, segregated witness (SEGWIT) and hard forking are popular methods for scaling Layer 1 blockchains.

> · [sharding](https://www.theblock.co/learn/251462/what-is-sharding#:~:text=Sharding%20is%20a%20technique%20used,to%20enhance%20performance%20in%20general.)

Sharding is a mechanism for dividing a blockchain network into smaller, more manageable sections known as shards. Each shard operates separately, handling a share of the transaction effort and parallel processing significantly boosts the network's transaction capacity. By dividing the network into smaller sections, sharding allows it to function as a whole, reducing bottlenecks and increasing transaction speed.

> · [segregated witness](https://river.com/learn/what-is-segwit/#:~:text=SegWit%20fixes%20transaction%20malleability%20by,used%20to%20unlock%20the%20bitcoin.)

SegWit (segregated witness) is a vital scaling solution for the Bitcoin network. SegWit splits transaction signatures by changing how data is stored. This frees considerable space, as these signatures account for around 70% of a transaction’s size. With signature data deleted, SegWit allows more transactions to fit into a single block, enhancing blockchain capacity and storage efficiency.

> · [hard forks](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/trading-investing/hard-fork)

A fork substantially modifies a blockchain network's structure or essential functionalities. It can change the size of the blocks or lessen the time required to create them. Hard forks are critical for increasing the scalability of layer one blockchains. However, one sort of hard fork, known as a controversial hard fork, causes a network split. In a controversial hard fork, a group of users deviates from the leading network to significantly modify the underlying code, frequently owing to conflicts with the core community's choices.

> · [layer 2 (off-chain) solutions](https://www.solulab.com/blockchain-layer-1-vs-layer-2-scaling-solutions/#:~:text=Layer%2D2%20Scaling%3A%20Layer%2D,without%20modifying%20the%20underlying%20protocol.)

Layer 2 scalability solutions are off-chain technologies that work with current blockchains. They enable transactions to be executed outside the main blockchain, decreasing congestion and increasing performance.

> · [side chains](https://www.coindesk.com/learn/an-introduction-to-sidechains/)

Sidechains are a viable option for tackling scalability difficulties with blockchains. They function as separate transactional chains alongside the main blockchain, making them ideal for processing large volumes of transactions. Sidechains use separate consensus methods from the main chain, allowing for scalability and transaction performance improvements. Utility tokens are widely used as part of the mechanics of side chains to ease data transmission between them and leading chains. Within this system, the main blockchain is mainly responsible for general security and dispute resolution. Furthermore, it is critical to understand that side chains differ dramatically from state channels in various ways. Remember that sidechain transactions are not private because they are recorded on the public ledger and also that security breaches on sidechains will not impact the main chain or other sidechains. Nonetheless, building up a sidechain demands tremendous work since you must start from zero. The security of side chains relies on their specific consensus mechanisms and security measures.

> · [plasma](https://ethereum.org/en/developers/docs/scaling/plasma/)

Plasma is a blockchain scaling solution specifically intended for Ethereum. It employs[ child chains](https://www.jelurida.com/resources/child-chains-and-side-chains) that branch out from the main [Ethereum blockchain](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/e/ethereum.asp). These subsidiary chains function independently, processing transactions outside of the main chain. This strategy helps reduce congestion and boost Ethereum's scalability by dispersing transactions over many chains. Specialised processing requirements can be handled by establishing isolated and secure environments known as plasma chains. These chains can support specialised applications and smart contracts, providing smooth transaction processing. Transactions completed on these sub-chains are finalised and recorded on the leading Ethereum network, resulting in quicker and more efficient transaction processing.

> · [delegated proof of stake](https://www.gemini.com/cryptopedia/proof-of-stake-delegated-pos-dpos#section-delegated-proof-of-stake)

DPOS (delegated proof of stake) is a decision-making method comparable to how countries choose their presidents. In DPOS, token holders choose validators to supervise network transactions. The number of validators might range from 10 to 100, which varies regularly.

> · [proof of authority](https://www.fool.com/terms/p/proof-of-authority/)

Proof of authority is an alternative blockchain consensus process based on reputation. Unlike [proof of work ](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/p/proof-work.asp)and [proof of stake](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/p/proof-stake-pos.asp), proof of authority utilises several nodes validating transactions. These nodes serve as administrators and can manage the blockchain. To participate, individuals must stake their identities, which necessitates a robust screening procedure to assure the validity of validators.

> · [payment channels](https://www.horizen.io/academy/payment-channels/#:~:text=Payment%20channel%20networks%20are%20built,built%20on%20Ethereum%20is%20Raiden.)

Unlike conventional blockchain transactions, payment channels enable users to send off-chain payments to one another without passing via the leading network. Parties can set up a payment channel, conduct as many transactions as they like, and then terminate it to record the final balance on the blockchain. This off-chain technique speeds up, reduces costs, and improves transaction privacy. Payment channels include[ Bitcoin's Lightning Network ](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/l/lightning-network.asp)and[ Ethereum's Raiden Network.](https://www.bitdegree.org/crypto/learn/crypto-terms/what-is-raiden-network#:~:text=Meaning%3A,doesn't%20require%20global%20consensus.)

Without doubt, the rising popularity of blockchain applications highlights the need to solve scalability challenges. As more users use blockchain networks and perform transactions, the network may get crowded, slowing down transaction processing. Numerous reasons contribute to blockchain scalability issues and so, to address these difficulties, a variety of blockchain scaling solutions have been created. These solutions, which comprise layer one and layer two techniques, provide realistic strategies for overcoming scalability challenges and ensuring the seamless operation of blockchain networks. The path forward demands relentless research and development to strike the right balance between scalability, security and decentralization. However, will these advancements herald a new era of blockchain efficiency and adoption, and can the industry overcome its current limitations to unlock the full potential of decentralized finance? The future of blockchain technology hinges on resolving these pivotal questions, but are we ready to embrace this transformative journey?
