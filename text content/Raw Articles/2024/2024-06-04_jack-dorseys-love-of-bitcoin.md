# Jack Dorsey’s love of Bitcoin

Jack Dorsey was the co-founder of X (Twitter) which he sold to Elon Musk in 2022 for [$44billion](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/timeline-elon-musks-tumultuous-twitter-acquisition-attempt/story?id=86611191). Shortly after, Dorsey launched Bluesky, a decentralized social medium he hoped to be everything that Twitter was not. However, in the early weeks of May 2024, Dorsey left Bluesky [maintaining](https://www.businessinsider.com/jack-dorsey-bluesky-twiiter-nostr-interview-2024-5#:~:text=Jack%20Dorsey%20says%20he%20quit%20Bluesky%20because%20it%20was%20becoming%20another%20Twitter&text=Twitter%20cofounder%20Jack%20Dorsey%20helped,all%20the%20mistakes%22%20Twitter%20made.) it was repeating all the mistakes Twitter had made, especially as it had rolled out moderation tools and was becoming highly centralized. But Bluesky is not Dorsey’s first venture into decentralization - for a long time he has been an advocate of blockchain technology. Founded by Jack Dorsey and Jim McKelvey in 2009, what was Square has now changed its [name to Block](https://squareup.com/us/en/press/square-changes-name-to-block). It [quickly became a](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/tech/021017/square.asp#:~:text=In%202009%2C%20Jack%20Dorsey%20and,Square%20rebranded%20as%20Block%2C%20Inc.) _“leading platform for merchant services and mobile payments, serving millions of small businesses by offering credit card processing, sales tracking, and financing solutions.”_ The change from Square to Block was in 2021, with the new name reflecting a range of services and hinting at its interest in blockchain technology. Square, Cash App and its other services all remain under the Block umbrella, continuing to operate independently.

Dorsey sees Bitcoin as a technology that can disrupt the financial system, believing that Bitcoin is undervalued and has the potential to [reach $1 million by 2030](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/top-cryptocurrency-could-reach-1-095000959.html); and he argues that Bitcoin's ecosystem will make it more valuable. Block takes 10% of its gross profit [buying additional BTC every month](https://cointelegraph.com/news/block-shares-rise-q1-earnings-beat-estimates), making it among the first publicly-traded companies to directly invest in the cryptocurrency (purchasing $220 million worth of Bitcoin between late [2020 and early 2021](https://cointelegraph.com/news/jack-dorsey-block-invest-bitcoin-every-month-earnings-call)). In the same vein, Block [launched a new feature called Bitcoin Conversions](https://fortune.com/crypto/2024/04/25/jack-dorsey-block-firm-square-bitcoin-conversions/) so allowing Square merchants to automatically convert a portion of their daily sales (1-10%) into Bitcoin and transfer it to their cash app. Dorsey sees Bitcoin as more than just a technological innovation; it represents an investment in a future where financial empowerment becomes the norm. And, despite initial scepticism, Block reports strong merchant interest and sees this as another way to connect Bitcoin with mainstream commerce. As of March 31st, Block held 8,038 Bitcoin - valued at roughly $573 million. This represents a significant paper gain of $233 million on its initial investment. However, “ _[less than 3% of company resources are dedicated to Bitcoin-related projects.](https://www.digitaltransactions.net/block-slams-news-reports-of-a-doj-investigation-for-compliance-violations/)_[”](https://www.digitaltransactions.net/block-slams-news-reports-of-a-doj-investigation-for-compliance-violations/) __ In 2018, Bitcoin was just over $9,000 when Dorsey made [the prediction of it being the internet’s single currency](https://www.cnbc.com/2018/03/21/jack-dorsey-expects-bitcoin-to-become-the-worlds-single-currency-in-about-10-years.html). Now in 2024, it hovers around $70,000. His recent [$ million prediction](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/top-cryptocurrency-could-reach-1-095000959.html) is shared by many other such as [Robert Kiyosaki](https://news.bitcoin.com/rich-dad-poor-dad-author-robert-kiyosaki-believes-bitcoin-price-will-reach-2-3-million/#:~:text=Rich%20Dad%20Poor%20Dad%20author%20Robert%20Kiyosaki%20says%20he%20believes,the%20U.S.%20to%20go%20bankrupt.) \- others might feel like it is rather optimistic. But Jack Dorsey can be said to have ‘walked his talk’; that is - putting his money where his heart is.

Back in December 2023, Block launched its [Bitkey Bitcoin wallet](https://cointelegraph.com/news/jack-dorsey-block-launches-self-custody-bitcoin-wallet). And on April 23rd, it made a big push into Bitcoin mining by [developing a new, highly efficient](https://www.mining.build/latest-updates-3nm-system/) 3-nanometer mining chip, continuing on with building a complete [Bitcoin mining system](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2024/04/24/jack-dorseys-block-is-building-a-bitcoin-mining-system/) based on the chip design to help ease challenges faced by miners after the Bitcoin halving, which cut rewards by 50%. This move aims to address two key issues; **decentralization** \- by offering alternative mining options, Block hopes to reduce the industry's reliance on a few dominant players, and **efficiency** \- the new chip technology promises faster mining speeds and lower costs for miners, especially after the recent Bitcoin halving mentioned above. Notably, this could significantly impact the entire Bitcoin mining landscape. A Bitcoin miner, [Gridless](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/04/20/bitcoin-miner-gridless-backed-by-block-builds-site-at-kenya-volcano.html) (backed by Jack Dorsey), uses renewable energy sources, a mix of solar power and the stranded wasted energy to power Bitcoin mining in three African countries, including a Bitcoin mining facility located near a Kenyan volcano. Whilst cryptocurrency has been often criticised for its energy intensive nature, this company uses stranded geothermal waste energy in Kenya to power its mining system and bring electricity to rural homes in Kenya, Malawi and Zambia. Bitcoin mining can be very volatile for miners as well as the volatility caused by the speculative nature of the market, which was recently influenced partly by the halving event and adoption of Bitcoin ETFs. Essentially, the halving event leaves far [fewer Bitcoin blocks to be mined,](https://cryptopotato.com/bitcoin-miners-report-declines-in-production-as-profitability-slumps/) and hence fewer rewards. Currently, it is at 3.125 BTC per block, having dropped from 6.25 BTC; many [miners are consolidating to sell later](https://cryptopotato.com/bitcoin-sees-longest-miner-consolidation-period-since-trading-near-16k/).

Dorsey is also a believer in a new generation of the internet but is dissatisfied with the current trajectory of Web3. No surprise that he proposes an alternative vision - [Web5](https://medium.com/@DigitalVisionBrands/jack-dorseys-concept-of-web5-how-does-it-evolve-from-web3-a6e4966a5ba8). And, unlike Web 3, which seeks to build a decentralized web on entirely new infrastructure, Web5 leverages the existing internet. This approach aims for faster adoption and integration and, more importantly, Web5 prioritises Bitcoin as the foundational element, believing its established network and security are superior to those of newer blockchains. This focus on Bitcoin aligns with Block's deep investment in the cryptocurrency and its mining ecosystem since Dorsey argues that Web3 is [dominated by Venture Capitalists](https://thedefiant.io/news/defi/dorseys-caustic-tweet-sparks-debate-about-who-truly-owns-web3) (VCs). Whilst true, Dorsey’s company (Square) itself relied heavily on VC funding during its early stages. Secondly, Dorsey criticises [the lack of true decentralization in Web3](https://thedefiant.io/news/defi/dorseys-caustic-tweet-sparks-debate-about-who-truly-owns-web3); this is a valid point, as many Web3 projects continue to rely on centralized infrastructure. However, Dorsey has also faced criticism for his own centralized approach to platforms such as X (Twitter), where he held significant control over user experience. But Dorsey fears Web 3 could lead to a repeat of ‘big tech’ control which is ironic, considering the immense power tech giants such as X (Twitter) have wielded under his leadership. All these considerations raise different questions on Jack Dorsey and the wider Web3 industry; are there any other ways for the next internet to evolve? Whilst Web3 aims to distribute control and potentially profits to users, only time can tell whether VC’s engagement will prove profitable. The potential for Web3 is significant, given Citi Group estimates that by 2030 it will attract [5billion users and capture up to $13trillion](https://blockworks.co/news/citi-projects-metaverse-to-capture-five-billion-users-and-up-to-13b). Back in 2021, Dorsey claimed: “ _[Bitcoin will replace the US dollar and there is nothing more important to work on than Bitcoin”](https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/currencies/jack-dorsey-cardi-b-bitcoin-replace-dollar-web3-vc-influence-2021-12). _And, as reported by [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/digital-assets/2024/01/03/jack-dorsey-bitcoin-and-an-open-source-web-5-economy/?sh=209bf5a317a8):_“Dorsey believes that open-source development paired with bitcoin will enable everyone to participate in the future economy. He told me that he will do whatever it takes to realize an internet native currency in his lifetime. He sees bitcoin as the only option for permissionless money transmission and a tool for achieving that goal_.”

Love him or doubt him, Dorsey's bold bets on Bitcoin are shaking up the tech and finance world and, given that he is alleged to be worth over [$6.9billion](https://andsimple.co/cases/jack-dorsey-wealth/), would you want to bet against him? After all, how long did it take for you to open a Twitter account, or understand, let alone buy some Bitcoin….?
