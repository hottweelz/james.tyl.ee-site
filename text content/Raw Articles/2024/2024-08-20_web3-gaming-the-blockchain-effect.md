# Web3 gaming: the blockchain effect on the gaming industry

Gaming is often in the vanguard of technological evolution, with the industry witnessing a dramatic change at the crossing point of immersive virtual worlds and blockchain technology. As a result of the emergence of Web3 and the introduction of blockchain-powered platforms, gaming experiences are being revolutionised - showcasing the potential of decentralisation and expanding the adoption of play-to-pay and blockchain-based economic models. Over the years, the global gaming market has increased significantly and is expected to continue the trend. Currently, [3million+ monthly](https://x.com/AndrewAsksHow/status/1787783162996633831) active gaming makes up for approximately [30%](https://hackernoon.com/the-future-of-web3-games-prospects-and-dangers) of blockchain activity. [According to Global Market Insights](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/web3-gaming-market), the Web3 gaming market was valued at $26.38 billion in 2023 and is projected to experience significant growth, registering a CAGR of over 19.2% from 2024 to 2032. The numbers of gamers is massive, with PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds (PUBG)**** having reached over [100million.](https://plarium.com/en/blog/popular-games/#:~:text=again%20and%20again.-,PlayerUnknown's%20Battlegrounds%20\(PUBG\),enjoying%20this%20Battle%20Royale%20classic.) And much of this substantial upturn can be attributed to the use of blockchains and the invention of NFTs which ensure security, transparency and absolute ownership of in-game assets.

Blockchain, with its decentralised ledger technology[, offers unique benefits](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbesbusinesscouncil/2024/07/09/the-future-of-web3-games-analysis-and-perspective/) to the gaming industry; it provides transparency through peer hosting, whereby ensuring all players have access to the data whilst eliminating a centralised third party. In addition, blockchain-based gaming gives players ownership and control over their in-game assets, such as currencies and virtual land; the technology equips gamers with greater control over their gaming experience, allowing digital assets to be traded or used across different games. In addition, these assets can have real-world value. For example, some players have reportedly surpassed traditional job incomes [through their engagements](https://naavik.co/deep-dives/axie-infinity/) on [Axie Infinity](https://axieinfinity.com/). Decentralised networks create innovative in-game economies where gamers can generate income whilst playing, and earn real money for their achievements. In Web3 games, players are often rewarded with cryptocurrency or tokens for their in-game activities through the ‘play-to-earn’ model, so enabling them to monetise their time and skills. These games also offer [interoperability](https://cointelegraph.com/blockchain-for-beginners/what-is-blockchain-interoperability-a-beginners-guide-to-cross-chain-technology) with other decentralised applications (dApps), allowing NFTs obtained in one game to be utilised in a different game. Furthermore, blockchain provides community governance - an aspect of games involving players in decision-making processes, such as voting on updates and development proposals. Blockchain-based games are also designed to minimise cheating and hacking risks by ensuring that recorded transactions are unalterable. By printing more copies of rare items or giving them out as rewards, developers of traditional games can mount [inflationary pressure](https://cointelegraph.com/news/inflation-got-you-down-5-ways-to-accumulate-crypto-with-little-to-no-cost) on the in-game economy. Conversely, blockchains can ensure digital scarcity, making in-game assets valuable, unique and rare.

Undoubtedly, the general perception of gaming has changed with the birth of Web3, which introduces decentralisation and transparency with immersive gaming experience. And by offering a wide range of advantages, Web3 has attracted more attention to the gaming industry. Additionally, blockchain funds and VC corporations are pouring capital into helping game developers accelerate the development process, thus bringing new Web3 games faster to the market. Yet, although the industry continues to innovate, one cannot help to wonder about future development possibilities - i.e., what are the trends that would significantly influence Web3 gaming? Certainly, in the short term, as [play-to-earn (P2E](https://101blockchains.com/play-to-earn-games/)) gains traction and more Web3 games adopt the model, there will be an increased influx of players drawn by the concept and chance of making real money from virtual economies. Meanwhile, the [metaverse](https://101blockchains.com/metaverse-technology/) stands out as another key trend in Web3 gaming's future; Web3 games can tap into the metaverse to create immersive and interconnected gaming worlds. And, by leveraging the ability of the metaverse to connect virtual and real worlds, Web3 gaming will bloom even more. Furthermore, it is expected that, as traditional gaming studios turn to blockchain technology to explore its benefits, major gaming companies will adopt the technology in various aspects of their games to offer true ownership, decentralisation and community-oriented economies.

[According to experts](https://101blockchains.com/future-of-web3-gaming/), in-game assets might extend beyond the identity of a digital certificate in the future. It (what?????) can be the connection between real and virtual economies, stimulating and promoting transformative changes in community engagement. After all, it is already seven years ago that McLaren became the [first F1 team](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0JOxYZT0p2U) to enter e-games, having become, since then, a significant revenue earner. And, in addition, having taken data from F1 cars to improve e-games experience, now [McLaren is offering NFTs.](https://cars.mclaren.com/gb-en/nft-faqs) Indeed, NFTs stand out as a key feature in Web3 games and they will assuredly play a key role in shaping what is next for these games. Another strong trend is [AAA games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AAA_\(video_game_industry\)), with the combination of these two trends set to cause a revolution in players’ perception and interaction with games. Essentially, AAA Web3 games using NFTs can offer gaming experiences that will overcome many of the existing roadblocks. The first on-lone game was [Huntercoin (in 2014](https://cointelegraph.com/sponsored/qorpo-gamefi-hub-demo/?p=3569#:~:text=First%20Major%20Success,25%25%20of%20Ethereum's%20network%20activity.)), but the really big breakthrough and global success was CryptoKitties (in 2017) which, at one stage, accounted for 25% of Ethereum’s blockchain activity. Other Web3 games with millions of players include [Decentraland](https://decentraland.org/), in which players can own virtual land and create experiences on their land, where these experiences can also be explored and traded; [Axie Infinity](https://axieinfinity.com/) is a play-to-earn metaverse game where players collect, raise and battle ‘Axies’ and has a huge number of gamers in the Philippines; [The Sandbox](https://www.sandbox.game/en/) allows player to build and monetise their virtual worlds including in-game assets and digital currencies; [DeFi Kingdoms](https://gam3s.gg/defi-kingdoms/) is a virtual role-playing game offering a P2E model for players with DeFi integration such as renting ‘heroes’ and staking. Gamers use heroes (avatars) which are NFTs and can be traded to fight enemies and earn new rewards. In addition, other top Web3 games include [PlayDoge](https://cryptonews.com/ext/playdoge/), [Gods Unchained](https://gam3s.gg/gods-unchained/), [Illuvium](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNDmtsFOJzw), [SpongeV2](http://cryptonews.com/ext/sponge), [Fight Night](https://cryptonews.com/ext/fightnight/) and Pixels, and with the continual arrival of new Web3 games, the list is inexhaustible. Notably, according to [Dappradar](https://dappradar.com/blog/state-of-blockchain-gaming-in-q2-2024): “ _Q2 2024 marks the best quarter since Q3 2022 for blockchain gaming investments, as $1.1 billion were raised marking a 314% increase since the previous quarter”._ However, whilst Web3 gaming offers undeniable benefits, it is not without challenges and its future depends on addressing certain challenges, such as:

> · **user experience**

The interaction with NFTs and decentralised applications may be too complex for players who are not [crypto-savvy](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbesbusinesscouncil/2024/07/09/the-future-of-web3-games-analysis-and-perspective/), affecting the gaming experience and preventing new players’ entry. Initially, Web3 games emphasised incentive models with less focus on the user experience, graphics and gameplay. But to grow on a large scale, there must be an emergence of more user-friendly gaming platforms and DApps which reduce the barrier to entry, leading to broader adoption and increased engagement.

> · **scalability**

One of the main obstacles faced by Web3 gaming is [scalability](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbestechcouncil/2023/09/22/how-web3-gaming-is-transforming-the-gaming-landscape-through-p2e-and-in-game-economies/). With decentralised applications (DApps) becoming more popular, existing blockchain technology limitations are becoming obvious; the slow speed of transactions and network congestion detract from what players expect in an immersive gaming experience.

> · **regulatory difficulty**

The Web3 game is dependent on cryptocurrency and blockchain which still face regulatory strain in some countries. For Web3 game projects, being required to deal with complicated legal issues in multiple jurisdictions whilst ensuring compliance with established laws and regulations is a great challenge.

Essentially, Web3’s combination of classic gaming with blockchain technology presents an innovative entertainment layer with real ownership and economy. The synergy enhances value distribution among players, governance and transparency whereby attracting attention and increasing investments in the gaming industry. Furthermore, game monetisation and smart tokenomics enrich players’ engagement via redistribution of monetary value in the ecosystem. But, whilst it is not without challenges, Web3 gaming is expected to experience continued growth and innovation as gamers become increasingly informed.
