# Gaming and cryptocurrency 

Are you one of the [3.32billion](https://explodingtopics.com/blog/number-of-gamers) who play on-line games? If so, you will probably be familiar with the play-to -earn games (P2E) which were worth[ $3292.73 million in 2022](https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/play-earn-nft-games-market-123250571.html?roistat_visit=1285335) and projected to reach more than $8800 million by 2028. The P2E model is a gaming framework that allows its users to play games and earn money when playing games on-line, and has the potential to increase user participation and retention since it incentivises players to spend more time playing. At the heart of the P2E concept is blockchain technology, with the encrypted technology being critical because it helps to address the challenges of fraud, so allowing users to deal directly with one another without the need for third party intermediaries, i.e. giving participants control of what the player of a game has earned online. One of the most successful and fastest growing P2E games is [Hamster Kombat](https://cryptonews.com/news/hamster-kombat-players-drive-boom-in-massage-gun-purchases.htm), with other cryptocurrency-based P2E models steadily rising in popularity including:

· **Axie Infinity -** one of the most popular play-to-earn games, players earn Smooth Love Potion (SLP) tokens by breeding and battling digital creatures known as Axies.

· **Lost Relics -** is an action-adventure RPG in which players may earn Enjin Coin (ENJ) and other non-fungible tokens (NFTs) by exploring dungeons and completing objectives.

· **Gods Unchained** \- is a trading card game in which players may win Ethereum-based tokens (such as ERC-20 assets) through matches and tournaments.

· **CryptoBlades** \- this blockchain-based RPG game allows players to earn Skill tokens by defeating foes and manufacturing things.

They are programmable, which allows developers to create smart contracts to manage game transactions whilst also promoting equality as well as the ability to create unique digital assets such as [non-fungible tokens (NFTs) ](https://www.investopedia.com/non-fungible-tokens-nft-5115211#:~:text=Non%2Dfungible%20tokens%20\(NFTs\),are%20stored%20in%20other%20places.)that serve as ownership evidence for items.

**Advantages of using blockchain technology for play to earn games:**

· **reduces cheating and fraud**

The transparent nature of blockchain-powered platforms can minimise the amount of fraud. [Smart contracts ](https://www.ibm.com/topics/smart-contracts#:~:text=Smart%20contracts%20are%20typically%20used,when%20predetermined%20conditions%20are%20met.)manage game rules and outcomes, assuring fairness so that both players and game creators may feel confident in what they are doing and creating. This concurrently improves the safety of a gaming environment, to the point that individuals trust their respective objectives.

  * **ownership and control**




Possession and control are the primary advantages of digital money in P2E. In contrast to traditional digital platforms where game creators’ control and manage in-game assets, a blockchain-based game transfers genuine ownership of gaming personnel and money to players through the use of NFTs and digital tokens that cannot be interchanged. The legitimacy of this ownership is maintained using blockchain technology as there is a record of all transactions between various parties.

  * **prospective value appreciation**




Cryptocurrencies featured in P2E games may appreciate when players accumulate digital assets and currencies during gameplay, resulting in higher value owing to market demand and scarcity. In this aspect, it makes gaming more enticing and interesting for gamers.

  * **community and government involvement**




Using cryptocurrencies in P2E games leads to strong community participation and governance. Game development decisions, such as updates, new features or in-game economies, are usually voted for by the token holders. Such a democratic model gives power to gamers who would like to see changes made in the games they spend time playing and earning from hence creating unity among members, as well as instilling in them a sense of belonging.

  * **global market avenues**




Given that cryptocurrencies allow for international transactions, P2E video games may seek a global market of both players and investors. Thus, increased availability boosts opportunities for game producers whilst also enhancing the possibility of gamers selling game assets on various platforms throughout the world.

**Hurdles that have to be overcome in the use of cryptocurrency in P2E models:**

· **volatility uncertainty**

Cryptos are notoriously volatile, with values potential to fluctuate substantially over short periods. This presents a challenge to P2E users who receive tokens as incentives. Players' rewards may be drastically devalued if the token's price decreases unexpectedly, affecting the perceived worth of their time and effort engaged in games.

  * **technical obscurity**




Most transactions in play-to-earn (P2E) settings use blockchain technology, which combines security and transparency. At the same time, the technology introduces additional challenges due to its inherent nature, such as scalability issues and extremely costly transactions, which can impede the seamless integration of cryptocurrencies into gaming environments, impacting onboarding rates and client satisfaction.

  * **adoption and accessibility**




To be effective, P2E models should attract a diverse group of players. Most individuals who may use them are unaware of digital cash, which is only of interest to a limited number. The process of purchasing, storing and spending digital money may be highly hard initially for people to understand so dissuading some participating P2E games.

  * **security worries**




To ensure that cryptocurrency transactions are safe and secure and consumers do not lose money as a result of hacking or fraud, strong security measures must be used. Specifically in the P2E gaming environment, where blockchains are widely utilised and users can be paid large sums of digital money for playing, security is critical. This is because, when someone breaks in and to steals from gamers, it is likely the gamers will not return or even report their loss to the police.

  * **balanced economics**




To ensure the long-term viability of P2E ecosystems, it is critical to achieve some sort of equilibrium that allows them to stay playable and appealing to investors as the price of tokens rises. It is hard for any P2E platform to exist without steady economic conditions since it risks volatility fluctuations caused by annoyance from players and hesitancy from users, which would undoubtedly diminish participation rates.

One of the ways of mitigating the above is to manage volatility through stability mechanisms. The volatility of cryptocurrency rates can have a significant impact on the revenues of P2E participants. To mitigate this risk, developers can incorporate stabilisation measures into their game ecosystems. Smart contracts are utilised to prevent unexpected price swings by automatically changing token payouts based on market conditions or locking how much tokens are worth for specific periods. Alternatively, instead of rewarding P2E gamers with a cryptocurrency players could be given stablecoins thus providing gamers with consistent revenue stability. Another way to reduce the volatility of the rewards being earnt is for P2E games to issue a variety of cryptos to diversify players’ exposure to one type of crypto. To tackle some of the technical challenges such as capacity and high fees P2E games could create on-line games using Layer 2 blockchain solutions or [sidechains](https://shardeum.org/blog/sidechain/). Initially, [the user experience (UX)](https://thedatascientist.com/blockchain-and-ui-ux-design-ensuring-security-and-user-trust/#:~:text=Understanding%20Blockchain%20Technology,across%20a%20network%20of%20computers.) for those engaging with blockchain technology can be daunting, so it is essential to improve the adoption and accessibility of P2E models. For new players especially, it is important that attention is paid to educating and helping to improve their knowledge - [Gamification](https://b2broker.com/news/understanding-gamefi-future-of-the-gaming-landscape/#:~:text=It%20represents%20the%20concept%20of,rewards%20from%20playing%20video%20games.) is a powerful tool that can be deployed so that players can learn and be paid whilst playing. Finally,**** partnering with traditional gaming platforms, exchanges or payment gateways to make cryptocurrencies more accessible in P2E ecosystems. But unfortunately, nefarious actors have targeted P2E game. Although at its peak, Axie had over [2.7million users](https://prioridata.com/data/axie-infinity-users/), one of its biggest losses was [$600million,](https://playtoearngames.com/news/axie-infinity-loses-600-million-hack-attack) strongly intimating that security audits and penetration testing**** is vital.**** That is where smart contract audits, platform and wallet security audits help to identify and address vulnerabilities; muti-signature wallets and cold storage (i.e. keeping P2E rewards off-line)**** safeguard participants' token holdings. As the P2E market matures, it is increasingly possible to purchase insurance for games in the case of security breaches or losses due to hacking or fraud; play-to-earn games' long-term success demands a solid and sustainable economic model. Tokenomics that consider supply-demand dynamics, currency usefulness and inflation rates to provide a stable token value and long-term benefits, are vital.**** Transparent and easy to understand governance**** allows players to have a say on how financial matters are handled, together with the issuance of tokens by a community. The latest iteration of P2E is [Tap-2-Earn](https://www.coingecko.com/learn/what-is-tap-2-earn-crypto-top-tap-to-earn-games), such as Notcoin, Hamster Kombat, TapSwap, Pixelverse, Catizen, YesCoin and MemeFi. ‘Tap-to-Pay’ games, also known as ‘Pay-to-Play’ or ‘Pay-to-Win’ games, are mobile or online games that encourage players to make in-game purchases to advance more quickly or gain competitive advantages.

P2E games typically use a freemium model, where the base game is free to download and play, but players can spend real money on virtual items, currency or other enhancements. These games have transformed the gaming business efficiently, mixing gaming with blockchain technology and so creating a new paradigm in which playing and earning are inextricably linked. Whilst these games offer accessibility and continuous updates funded by purchases, they can create imbalances and become expensive for frequent spenders. In addition, ethical concerns have been raised about their impact on children and potential for fostering addictive behaviours. Future trends in the industry are moving towards more transparent and player-friendly monetisation models, balancing revenue generation with fair play. As the landscape of free play-to-earn games changes, now is an exciting time to be both a gamer and an investor. So, keep exploring, scheming and playing!
