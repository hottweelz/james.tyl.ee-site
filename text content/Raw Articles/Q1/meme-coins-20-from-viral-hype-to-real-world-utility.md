# Meme coins 2.0: from viral hype to real-world utility

*By Jonny Fry | *

Meme coins have certainly been a defining phenomenon in the cryptocurrency market, capturing the attention of mainstream media and retail investors alike. Initially launched as a joke, Dogecoin’s meteoric rise was fuelled by endorsements from high-profile figures such as Elon Musk and the fervour of on-line communities. Furthermore, these coins gained traction for their humorous branding, accessibility and speculative appeal - investors were drawn to the possibility of quick gains, often disregarding the absence of underlying value or use cases. Even Trump has launched a meme coin, $Trump, unsurprisingly attracting [over 800,000 investors](https://www.axios.com/2025/01/19/trump-meme-coin-what-to-know) and which has risen in value - reportedly at one stage having made his holding worth a staggering [$58billion](https://www.axios.com/2025/01/19/donald-trump-crypto-billionaire), which would account for 89% of Trump’s wealth! However, it is important to note that the actual money Trump makes from $Trump _will_ ultimately depend on its price as well as Trump being able to sell his holding. Let us wait and see….. Nevertheless, given the headlines and attention $Trump has created, many more will attempt to emulate Tump and join the [13million different meme coins](https://www.forbes.com/sites/daniellechemtob/2024/11/13/forbes-daily-meme-coins-are-cryptos-wildest-moneymaking-craze/) which have already been launched. Moreover, the speculative nature of meme coins has also made them highly volatile and susceptible to market trends - many investors experienced significant losses during market downturns as the value of meme coins plummeted without a foundation of intrinsic utility to sustain them. Furthermore, the limitations of meme coins, including their lack of scalability, practical use cases and reliance on hype, has eventually led to a growing realisation that sustainable value in cryptocurrencies requires more than merely viral popularity.

Meanwhile, as the cryptocurrency market has matured, the focus has begun to shift toward coins and tokens that offer tangible utility within specific ecosystems. Utility coins are digital assets designed to facilitate functions such as accessing services, executing smart contracts or participating in [decentralised applications (dApps)](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/d/decentralized-applications-dapps.asp) but, unlike meme coins, utility coins are tied to real-world use cases so making them more resilient and attractive to institutional investors and developers. Certainly, the emergence of utility coins reflects the broader trend of blockchain technology being integrated into diverse industries. From finance and supply chain management to healthcare and gaming, utility coins are enabling innovative solutions that address real-world challenges. And, without doubt, this evolution potentially marks a pivotal moment in the cryptocurrency landscape as value-driven assets gain prominence over speculative investments. In addition, meme coins are being used in conjunction with AI and, according to [OSL.com](https://osl.com/academy/article/what-are-ai-memecoins-bringing-ai-utility-to-memecoins?utm_source=chatgpt.com), they are “ _adding real utility, creating a bridge between entertainment and practical use cases in the blockchain ecosystem.”_

**Factors driving the shift toward utility coins**

> · **increased institutional interest**

Institutional investors have played a significant role in steering the market toward value-driven cryptocurrencies. Unlike retail investors, institutions prioritise assets with clear use cases, regulatory compliance and long-term growth potential. [Utility coins,](https://www.coinsmart.com/articles/what-are-utility-tokens/) with their focus on functionality and integration into blockchain ecosystems, align with these priorities. As a result, institutional capital has increasingly flowed into projects that emphasise real-world utility, accelerating the transition away from meme coins.

> · **regulatory developments**

The evolving regulatory landscape has also influenced the shift toward utility coins. Governments and regulatory bodies worldwide have begun to scrutinise cryptocurrencies more closely, particularly those perceived as speculative or fraudulent. Utility coins, which often adhere to stricter compliance standards and provide clear documentation of their use cases, are better positioned to navigate regulatory challenges. This has led to greater confidence among investors and developers in pursuing utility-driven projects.

> · **technological advancements**

Advancements in blockchain technology have expanded the potential applications of cryptocurrencies, driving interest in utility coins. Innovations such as [layer-2 scaling solutions](https://www.starknet.io/blog/layer-2-scaling-solutions/), [cross-chain interoperability](https://www.bitcoin.com/get-started/what-is-cross-chain-interoperability/) and improved [smart contract functionality](https://www.ibm.com/topics/smart-contracts#:~:text=Smart%20contracts%20are%20typically%20used,when%20predetermined%20conditions%20are%20met.) have made it possible to develop more complex and efficient blockchain ecosystems. Utility coins serve as the backbone of these ecosystems, powering [decentralised finance (DeFi)](https://www.investopedia.com/decentralized-finance-defi-5113835), [non-fungible tokens (NFTs)](https://aws.amazon.com/web3/nfts-explained/) and other applications that require seamless and secure transactions.

> · **market maturation**

The cryptocurrency market has matured significantly over the past decade with investors becoming more discerning about the assets they support. The initial frenzy surrounding meme coins has given way to a more measured approach, where investors prioritise projects with clear roadmaps, competent teams and sustainable value propositions. This maturation has created a fertile environment for utility coins to thrive.

Utility coins are at the forefront of numerous blockchain applications, driving innovation and transforming traditional industries. One notable example is their role in decentralised finance. Utility coins such as Ethereum’s Ether (ETH) enable users to interact with DeFi platforms, providing services such as lending, borrowing and trading without intermediaries. These coins serve as both a medium of exchange and a mechanism for executing smart contracts, making them indispensable to the DeFi ecosystem. In the gaming industry, utility coins power play-to-earn models and virtual economies. Projects such as [Axie Infinity](https://www.investopedia.com/what-is-axie-infinity-5220657) use native tokens to reward players, facilitate in-game transactions and enable ownership of digital assets. This integration of blockchain and gaming has created new revenue streams for players and developers, demonstrating the transformative potential of utility coins. Supply chain management is another area where utility coins are making an impact. By leveraging blockchain’s transparency and immutability, utility coins can facilitate real-time tracking of goods and enhance trust between supply chain participants. For instance, [VeChain’s native token, VET](https://vechain.org/), is used to verify product authenticity and streamline logistics processes. However, whilst utility coins offer significant advantages over meme coins, they are not without challenges, including:

> · **scalability -** this remains a critical issue, as blockchain networks must handle increasing transaction

volumes without compromising speed or security.

> · **high gas fees -** on networks such as Ethereum these have highlighted the need for more efficient

solutions, spurring the development of alternatives such as Solana and Binance Smart Chain.

> · **regulatory uncertainty** \- this continues to pose risks for utility coin projects. As governments seek to establish comprehensive frameworks for digital assets, projects must navigate a complex and evolving landscape to remain compliant. This requires significant resources and expertise, which may be challenging for smaller teams.

Yet, despite these challenges, the opportunities for utility coins are immense. As blockchain adoption grows, the demand for coins that enable real-world applications will continue to rise. Collaboration between blockchain developers, industry leaders and policymakers will be crucial for overcoming obstacles and unlocking the full potential of utility coins. The growing awareness of meme coins and utility coins signals a broader evolution in the cryptocurrency market. As the industry matures, value-driven assets are likely to dominate, driven by the increasing integration of blockchain technology into everyday life; the future of these digital assets will be defined by projects that prioritise utility, sustainability and scalability. In this new era also, interoperability between blockchain networks will be a key focus - utility coins that facilitate seamless communication and collaboration between different ecosystems will play a central role in driving adoption. Additionally, the rise of [decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs)](https://www.avast.com/c-what-is-dao) and tokenised economies will create new opportunities for utility coins to empower communities and incentivise participation. Education and awareness will also be critical for fostering adoption; as more people understand the benefits of utility coins, the industry will gain broader acceptance and legitimacy. But this will require ongoing efforts to demystify blockchain technology and showcase its practical applications. By focusing on value creation and real-world impact, the industry is poised to enter a period of sustained growth and innovation. Traditional markets and industries ought to take note - meme coins and utility coins have been shown to engage in global on-line communities and are therefore possibly giving a glimpse on the shape of future finance, whereby offering valuable insights. Alternatively, are these digital assets an incarnation and merely represent the ethereal nature of some digital assets that can be created at the press of a button but will wither and fade in time?
