# AI, blockchain and the future of asset management: disrupting markets or re-inventing finance?

*By Jonny Fry | *

The integration of artificial intelligence into asset management is revolutionising financial strategies by enhancing efficiency and accuracy in decision-making. As highlighted by the CFA Institute, [AI has the potential to improve asset return predictions](http://www.cfainstitute.org/sites/default/files/-/media/documents/book/rf-lit-review/2020/rflr-artificial-intelligence-in-asset-management.pdf), refine risk assessments and solve intricate portfolio challenges. Furthermore, unlike traditional methods, this innovation is already transforming how investments are managed, portfolios are structured and markets operate. AI’s incorporation into asset management has enabled firms to analyse massive volumes of financial data at speeds far beyond human capability. [By leveraging machine learning, these systems identify trends in historical data, forecast asset movements and enhance portfolio allocation strategies](https://www.cfainstitute.org/sites/default/files/-/media/documents/book/rf-lit-review/2020/rflr-artificial-intelligence-in-asset-management.pdf), ultimately improving performance beyond traditional methods. This shift is already visible across the investment landscape, from hedge funds to retail trading platforms. There are many AI-powered [virtual protocol](https://www.coingecko.com/learn/what-is-virtuals-protocol-how-to-buy-trade-and-create-ai-agents)s, representing a cutting-edge fusion of AI and blockchain technology, whereby allowing users to co-own, govern and monetise tokenised and so challenge traditional financial payment providers. But unlike traditional AI applications that primarily focus on automation, this protocol embeds AI directly into financial ecosystems, giving it an active role in economic activity. Interest in AI-driven cryptocurrencies is evident in the growth of the virtual protocol and its deflationary model and potential for strong returns underscore the broader impact of blockchain and AI on financial markets. Nonetheless, this emerging space also faces challenges such as price fluctuations, regulatory scrutiny and adoption resistance. In addition, the ongoing evolution of the virtual protocol brings up crucial questions, such as: can AI’s capabilities in creating, managing and governing assets eventually compete with traditional financial institutions? And, will ethical concerns and regulatory frameworks pose limitations to its growth? Certainly, the emergence of AI-powered token ecosystems underscores the importance of not only technological progress but also the establishment of transparent and accountable guidelines. However, hailed as the next ‘big thing’ in digital assets, AI agent tokens experienced a surgence in popularity through 2024 - only to reveal a brutal truth: most do not survive beyond the hype. A [staggering 88% of these tokens](https://news.bitcoin.com/the-ai-agent-token-bubble-why-75-of-traders-are-losing-money/) are already dead, with an average lifespan of just __ 17 days. According to a study by Phut Crypto, far from having a revolution, many now resemble short-lived speculative bubbles, leaving 75% of traders in the red. And this raises a critical question: are AI agent tokens genuine innovations or simply financial mirages? Furthermore, whilst AI itself is reshaping industries, its tokenised counterparts seem more akin to experiments in market psychology - designed to lure traders into cycles of fleeting euphoria. So, as blockchain and AI continue to converge, will we see tokens with real utility or are we simply witnessing the latest chapter in crypto’s history of unsustainable hype?

However, AI's role in trading digital assets is growing rapidly, with various platforms leveraging machine learning to enhance trading efficiency and market intelligence - above is a summary of notable AI-powered trading platforms. In addition, the role of AI in asset management goes beyond mere efficiency improvements; these AI-powered platforms are opening up advanced financial tools to retail investors, providing them with the ability to compete with institutional asset managers. However, this development introduces critical challenges regarding regulation, fairness and transparency. As AI’s influence over financial decisions increases, the need for oversight and ethical considerations becomes more pressing. Furthermore, regulators must grapple with crucial issues, such as: how to hold autonomous AI agents accountable for their financial decisions; what regulatory frameworks are needed to ensure compliance for AI-based trading platforms; and, how to curb market manipulation driven by AI-generated trading strategies. The challenges of manipulation and potential unintended consequences that could impact financial markets has been highlighted by Jonathan Hall from the Bank of England in his speech, [“Monsters of the Deep”.](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/speech/2024/may/jon-hall-speech-at-the-university-of-exeter) Without doubt, the regulatory landscape is evolving, but the one clear takeaway is that AI is rapidly reshaping asset management.

AI-driven digital assets such as virtuals protocol are gaining significant traction, illustrating the increasing demand for AI agents in the crypto space. The Virtuals token, VIRTUAL, recently surged to a [record high of $1.45, raising its market capitalisation to $1.45 billion](https://www.banklesstimes.com/articles/2024/11/29/virtuals-protocol-hits-1-4b-as-ai-agents-gain-traction-in-crypto/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) \- this remarkable growth underscoring the growing faith investors have in AI-enhanced blockchain technologies. AI agents which are, in effect, software programs that automate and execute tasks such as trading, market analysis and portfolio management, are becoming integral to decentralised finance. Some AI-driven tokens have witnessed extraordinary gains, including [Goatseus Maximus (GOAT)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jFrqSLtKy0Q) and [Act I: The AI Prophecy (ACT),](https://coinmarketcap.com/currencies/act-i-the-ai-prophecy/) both of which skyrocketed over 1,000% following Binance listings. VIRTUAL also demonstrated impressive momentum, surging 153.8% in just seven days, signalling strong market enthusiasm. So, could AI-powered asset management principles find their place beyond the world of cryptocurrencies? The “verticals” concept is where AI oversees trading and governance, might be adapted to traditional financial instruments such as equities and bonds. If so, this could reshape capital markets, fostering enhanced levels of efficiency, accessibility and transparency. There are currently over [55,000 publicly listed companies worldwide](https://focus.world-exchanges.org/issue/february-2024/market-statistics), with more than 31,000 of them on Asian exchanges. However, it is estimated that around [390 million businesses operate globally, many of which face challenges in accessing capital through traditional methods](https://www.brimco.io/business-statistics-to-know/#:~:text=There%20are%20an%20estimated%20359,when%20there%20were%20314.21%20million). Tokenisation offers an attractive solution by providing continuous market access and democratising investment opportunities but, unlike traditional stock exchanges with set trading hours, tokenised shares could be traded 24/7, boosting market efficiency. Investors worldwide could easily buy and sell tokenised equities without the need for intermediaries such as brokers, therefore reducing friction and costs. Moreover, smart contracts could automate dividend distributions and enable real-time settlements, greatly enhancing liquidity and operational efficiency. This compelling scenario possibly explains why [Larry Fink, CEO of BlackRock](https://crypto.news/blackrock-ceo-larry-fink-calls-for-tokenized-stocks-and-bonds/#:~:text=BlackRock%20CEO%20Larry%20Fink%20appeared,U.S.%20clients%2C%20underscores%20this%20approach.) (the world’s largest asset manager) is calling for the tokenisation of funds, equities and debt instruments.

Certainly, as AI-driven asset management evolves, then integrating these principles into traditional markets could redefine how financial assets are issued, traded and managed whereby bridging the gap between legacy finance and digital innovation. By integrating AI into smart contracts, equity and bond management could be revolutionised through increased automation, transparency and efficiency in financial markets (these intelligent contracts streamline critical functions, eliminating manual processes and reducing both costs and operational risks). Shareholder voting could be significantly improved through blockchain-powered governance, so creating immutable records and promoting greater engagement. Additionally, AI algorithms could monitor real-time regulatory compliance, helping to prevent fraud and market manipulation risks. This shift would improve both market efficiency and accessibility, allowing retail and institutional investors to compete more fairly. In essence, with AI continuing to integrate with blockchain technology, financial markets are heading toward a future that is more automated, inclusive and transparent.

The intersection of AI and blockchain is no longer speculative - it is actively reshaping asset management and digital trading. Whether through AI-powered virtuals protocol or the tokenisation of traditional equities and bonds, this transformation is accelerating at an unprecedented pace. But, whilst progress is underway, challenges still exist. Regulatory uncertainties, ethical dilemmas and the potential for AI-driven market manipulation must be overcome. Collaboration among financial institutions, regulators and investors is essential to develop frameworks that ensure transparency, fairness and accountability.

So, the questions are not, _if_ AI-driven financial systems will take hold, but _when_ and _how_ regulators, exchanges and investors will navigate this transformation. Will we see the rise of fully autonomous financial ecosystems, or will human oversight remain a necessity in asset management? One thing is clear, though: AI and blockchain are rewriting the rules of finance - and those who adapt fastest will shape the future of global markets.
