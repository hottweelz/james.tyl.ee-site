# Meme coins: from joke to serious business

*By Jonny Fry | *

[Meme coins](https://www.investopedia.com/meme-coin-6750312) began as internet jokes, often designed to mock the seriousness of traditional cryptocurrencies, and are sometimes referred to as the internet’s wildest financial experiment. “ _A strange logic is driving this carnival: Meme culture, market sentiment, and technological narratives are redefining the value boundaries of Meme coins_ ” is how [Chaincatcher](https://www.chaincatcher.com/en/article/2160196) has described the situation. [Dogecoin](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/d/dogecoin.asp), the pioneer in this category, was created in 2013 by software engineers, Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer and was inspired by the popular “Doge” meme featuring a Shiba Inu dog. What set Dogecoin apart was its playful approach, contrasting with the often opaque and highly technical nature of other cryptocurrencies. And initially, Dogecoin was a fun way for internet users to tip each other or support charitable causes. However, its quirky identity and loyal community allowed it to survive long after many dismissed it as a novelty. Launched in 2020, Shiba Inu took the meme coin concept to the next level. Branding itself as the “Dogecoin killer”, Shiba Inu combined the viral appeal of internet memes with strategic tokenomics (such as a massive token supply and community-driven initiatives) to attract investors. These coins may have started as jokes, but their astronomical rise during bull markets suggests otherwise.

Meanwhile, the surge of meme coin prices is inextricably linked to the broader digital asset market dynamics and in Q4 meme coin prices escalated along with other digital assets, particularly following the re-election of Trump who, himself, is seen to want his new administration to take a more supportive stance to cryptocurrencies in general. Bull markets, characterised by rapid price increases, create an atmosphere of speculative frenzy. As Bitcoin and Ethereum hit all-time highs, investors are looking for the _“next big thing”_ \- meme coins often fill this gap due to their low entry price and viral potential. Notably, in the 2021 bull market, Dogecoin experienced a meteoric rise, climbing from fractions of a cent to an all-time high of $0.73, driven by a perfect storm of factors, including:

> · **celebrity endorsements** \- public figures such as Elon Musk and Mark Cuban tweeted enthusiastically about Dogecoin, fuelling its price surge. In particular, Musk styled himself as the “Dogefather”, cementing Dogecoin’s place in the public consciousness.
> 
> · **social media hype** \- platforms such as [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/?rdt=37092), Twitter and TikTok amplified the meme coin narrative, creating a sense of FOMO (fear of missing out) among retail investors.
> 
> · **retail speculation** \- with easy access to trading apps such as [Robinhood](https://robinhood.com/us/en/) and [Binance](https://www.binance.com/en), even casual investors jumped into the frenzy whereby further inflating prices.

Shiba Inu followed a similar trajectory, leveraging its branding as an underdog and fostering a community-driven ecosystem with projects such as [ShibaSwap ](https://shibaswap.com/)(a decentralised exchange) and plans for NFTs and gaming. However**,** what make meme coins so attractive to investors? The answer lies in a blend of accessibility, culture and speculative psychology. Meme coins often trade at fractions of a cent, giving the illusion that they are affordable investments with room for exponential growth. This is not unique to meme coins as penny shares have been well-known in equity markets for years. [Penny shares](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/investing/what-are-penny-stocks) are typically categorised as quoted companies that are very volatile, have a share price usually measured in pennies or cents, are under-researched and the shares have limited financial resources or track records, and the bid offer spread (i.e. the price between what you sell and buy a penny share) can be substantial. In addition, the prices of penny stock are often driven by large holders otherwise known as “whales”, who can drive the price up or down given the typical illiquidity of many penny shares. Hence, having read this list it is easy to see the similarities between penny shares and meme coins. The added factors that can fuel meme coin prices is that they trade 24/7, are typically driven by global online communities, huge FOMO, and what would appear to be a cultural phenomenon.

Certainly, the success of meme coins is deeply rooted in their communities which create a sense of belonging and purpose around holding these assets. The collective enthusiasm often resembles grassroots movements, amplified by memes, humour and camaraderie. Meme coins offer the fast huge returns, even if they lack real uses cases; this speculative appeal attracts both experienced traders and newcomers. Also, meme coins tap into the broader internet culture, combining fun, irony and rebellion against traditional financial norms. They resonate with younger, tech-savvy audiences who see them as more than just financial instruments - almost a hobby and, hopefully, as a way to get rich. But whilst the rise of meme coins has been entertaining and profitable for some, it is not without controversy. Critics argue that meme coins represent the excesses of the crypto market, often leading to financial losses for uninformed investors as they are driven by a variety of factors which are not necessarily conducive for long terms investing. These include:

> · **lack of utility** \- unlike projects that focus on [decentralised finance (DeFi)](https://www.britannica.com/money/decentralized-finance-defi), smart contracts or interoperability, most meme coins have little to no inherent utility. Their value is driven almost entirely by speculation.
> 
> · **volatility** \- meme coins often have little to no intrinsic value, so their prices can fluctuate dramatically based on [social media trends and hype](https://www.devx.com/crypto/the-volatility-factor/).
> 
> · **pump-and-dump schemes** \- the low cost and hype-driven nature of meme coins make them susceptible to manipulation. Unsuspecting investors can fall victim to [“pump-and-dump”](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/crypto-crime-2024-pump-and-dump/#:~:text=Pump%20and%20dump%20schemes%20typically,token's%20price%2C%20impacting%20unsuspecting%20holders.) schemes orchestrated by early adopters or influencers.
> 
> · **environmental concerns** \- [some meme coins rely on energy-intensive proof-of-work](https://vocal.media/theChain/meme-coins-internet-s-wildest-financial-experiment) algorithms, raising concerns about their environmental impact. Despite these criticisms, the meme coin phenomenon continues to evolve, challenging traditional notions of value and investment.

Values in prices have surged as meme coins have harnessed the power and enthusiasm for tens of millions of supporters/buyers, shaping perceptions and driving adoption in unexpected ways - they have left an indelible mark. MarketVector’s Meme Coin Index (which tracks the performance of the six largest meme coins) has soared [242% in 12 months](https://www.marketvector.com/indexes/digital-assets/marketvector-meme-coin) compared to Bitcoin’s [112% price rise](https://www.coingecko.com/en/coins/bitcoin). Furthermore, the trading volume generated by meme coins has boosted liquidity for digital exchanges and projects such Shiba Inu have pushed the [boundaries of tokenomics](https://sdlccorp.com/post/the-role-of-token-burns-in-cryptocurrency-deflation-shiba-inu-as-a-case-study/), exploring concepts such as deflationary mechanisms and community governance. Finally, the playful, irreverent nature of meme coins has inspired creative projects that blend entertainment, finance and technology, arguably making the topic of money and finances fun!

However, the future of meme coins remains uncertain. Will they evolve into legitimate projects with real-world applications, or will they fade into obscurity as the market corrects? Some argue that meme coins are a bubble waiting to burst, whilst others see them as a cultural and financial revolution. The likelihood is that the reality lies somewhere in between. Meme coins are indeed having an impact on the adoption of digital assets and their culture, and innovation is undeniable, engaging virally on-line with global communities. Moreover, as blockchain technology becomes more integrated into everyday life, meme coins could find niche applications, such as in gaming, social media or community-driven platforms.

In conclusion, once dismissed as internet jokes, meme coins have transformed into a cultural and financial phenomenon, redefining the way we view digital assets and leveraging the viral power of memes and community-driven enthusiasm to gain widespread attention. With their playful appeal and low entry cost, meme coins have introduced millions - especially younger, tech-savvy investor, often acting as a gateway to the broader blockchain ecosystem. However, their value is often detached from utility, driven instead by market sentiment and community hype. Critics point to their volatility, environmental impact and vulnerability to manipulation as significant risks, likening them to penny stocks in the traditional financial world. Beyond speculation, they have made conversations about finance and digital assets more accessible, even fun, engaging audiences who might otherwise have remained on the sidelines. Ultimately, as blockchain technology becomes more integrated into daily life, so could meme coins find lasting roles in gaming, social media and community-based platforms, leaving a legacy as both financial experiments and cultural milestones. In the end, the journey to date of meme coins is a reminder that value, like beauty, often lies in the eye of the beholder.
