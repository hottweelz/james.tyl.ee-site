# What to expect for Web3 going forward

*By Jonny Fry | *

So, what does the future hold for Web3? In 2024, we saw transformative growth in Web3 across gaming, politics and fashion. On Telegram, [Hamster Kombat](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/technology/gaming/hamster-kombat-suffers-a-drastic-drop-of-260-million-users-in-just-three-months/articleshow/114954483.cms) attracted over 300 million people only to lose 260 million a few months later - still, quite impressive being able to introduce 40+million to play on a blockchain-powered on-line game. Politically, the industry has increased its influence with crypto-friendly policies following President Trump’s re-election, pushing Bitcoin above $100,000. AI-powered Web3 applications have enhanced NFTs, DeFi and user experiences, whilst decentralised identifiers have empowered users to control digital identities - blockchain adoption has accelerated through enterprise use cases, government initiatives and retail interest. Globally meanwhile, standards are being agreed as to what a crypto asset is, driven by a desire to introduce international standards regarding taxation.

The reporting of any relevant crypto transaction will be the responsibility of reporting crypto asset service providers (RCASPs) and, according to [PwC](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/tax/newsletters/tax-policy-bulletin/assets/pwc-a-significant-milestone-global-implementation-of-the-carf.pdf),_“63 jurisdictions have committed to implementing a crypto-asset reporting framework (CARF) by 2027 or 2028._ ”

Specifics that need to be reported on include: details about the holder’s name, address, tax identification number, date of birth, as well as [RCASPs being required to provide particulars](https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/cryptoasset-reporting-framework-and-common-reporting-standard/cryptoasset-reporting-framework-and-amendments-to-the-common-reporting-standard-extension-to-domestic-reporting-and-implementation#:~:text=reporting%20Cryptoasset%20Service%20Providers%20\(%20RCASPs,jurisdiction%20where%20the%20taxpayer%20is) including:

> · the full name of the type of ‘relevant crypto asset’
> 
> · acquisitions and disposals of ‘relevant crypto assets’ against fiat currency
> 
> · acquisitions and disposals of “relevant crypto assets’ against other ‘relevant crypto assets’
> 
> · ‘reportable retail payment transactions’
> 
> · other ‘transfers of relevant crypto assets’ to and by the ‘reportable user’.

Meanwhile for Europe, the first phase of the EU’s Markets in Crypto Assets Regulation (MiCA) has commenced. The latest phase of MiCA introduces [significant changes for EU crypto regulation](https://www.forbes.com/sites/digital-assets/2024/12/03/what-you-should-know-about-the-latest-mica-regulations-coming-december/) that began on December 30, 2024. Crypto asset service providers (CASPs) must now meet strict authorisation, security and compliance requirements. New anti-money laundering and market manipulation rules will also take effect, although enforcement may not begin until 2026. Stablecoins face tighter restrictions, including volume limits and mandatory e-money licenses which could challenge smaller issuers. However, despite these hurdles, MiCA is seen as a positive framework to foster blockchain innovation whilst ensuring oversight. Retail users are largely unaffected and specific companies have been gearing up for the new MiCA regulations. Bitgit is [preparing for a Lithunia Hub](https://www.bakersfield.com/ap/news/bitget-prepares-for-mica-compliance-with-an-upcoming-european-regional-hub/article_b232308c-77aa-5ae5-8545-cb4b023bb7a5.html), and Revolut and PayPal are among companies engaging with Irish regulators to ensure they meet authorisation and governance standards - ultimately, firms need to register to avoid operational disruptions. Ireland is also [enforcing a 12-month transition](https://www.ccn.com/news/crypto/revolut-paypal-irish-regulator-mica-compliance/) for operational companies (shorter than the maximum 18 months) and non-compliance risks penalties or losing access to the EU market.

[In the US, 28% of adults (an estimated 65.7 million people](https://www.trtworld.com/magazine/trump-has-set-free-us-crypto-sector-and-it-could-be-a-global-game-changer-18261404)) own cryptocurrency today, a significant increase from only 15% in 2021 and, furthermore, off the back of being re-elected, Trump is seeking to make the US the crypto centre for the world. Various states are [preparing for a new digital asset framework](https://cryptoforinnovation.org/states-set-the-stage-for-2025-to-be-the-year-of-crypto/), with California's Assembly Bill 1934 at the forefront. Other states, such as Vermont and New Hampshire, are modernising money transmitter laws to include digital assets, whilst Maine has proposed restrictive measures on self-hosted wallets. In addition, some states, including Wyoming and Louisiana, are advancing legal frameworks for decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs). Certainly, this push for state-level regulation is representative of the zeitgeist, which is shifting toward establishing clear and robust digital asset policies for the future. And corporations will not be left behind in this move - a tier 1 FAANG institution will [likely adopt Bitcoin as a treasury asset.](https://www.forbes.com/sites/alexanderblume/2024/12/19/10-crypto-predictions-for-2025nations-corporations-regulations/) Michael Saylor of MicroStrategy has [already beckoned on Microsoft](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/12/10/michael-saylor-case-for-microsoft-buying-bitcoin-gets-rejected-.html) to do so. Likewise, Amazon shareholders have urged the company to adopt[ Bitcoin as a treasury asset to protect shareholder value](https://cryptopotato.com/amazon-shareholders-urge-bitcoin-adoption-as-treasury-asset-to-protect-value/).**** Whether they listen or not will be uncovered in 2025 but, if there is anything of which to be sure, it is that a tier 1 FAANG company will come too.

Meanwhile, Japan's upcoming tax plan will also be unveiled in 2025, including provisions for cryptocurrency assets. The Financial Services Agency (FSA) [plans to introduce a tax regime](https://www.ccn.com/news/crypto/japans-fsa-crypto-2025-tax-plan/) that lowers the current 55% tax rate on crypto gains to a flat 20% - this is part of a broader effort to make Japan's regulatory framework more favourable for Web3 businesses. Furthermore, through the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), the UK will [facilitate a consultation](https://cryptoslate.com/uk-aims-for-balanced-crypto-regulation-with-fca-led-consultation/) led by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). Being part of a broader effort to regulate crypto assets, this consultation ensures that the UK remains an attractive environment for digital asset innovation whilst addressing risks such as financial crime and investor protection; a draft regulation is expected in 2025, with full implementation of the [regime slated for 2026.](https://cryptoslate.com/fca-to-wait-till-2016-to-launch-official-crypto-policy-with-12-of-uk-owning-digital-assets/) In addition, by issuing a statutory, the [UK HMRC](https://www.financemagnates.com/cryptocurrency/uk-treasury-separates-staking-from-schemes-clarifying-blockchain-validation/) has confirmed that staking services are not collective investment schemes and, in doing so, have provided clear regulatory guidance - which is likely to attract more crypto staking companies to the UK.

In 2024, we saw two industries - Web3 and AI - cross paths and merge, meaning [AI agents will play more roles in Web3](https://bravenewcoin.com/insights/ai-agents-to-dominate-2025-bold-predictions-from-web3-leaders), particularly for staking and decentralised applications. These autonomous systems, already earning millions through on-chain activities, could exceed one million by 2025. And, despite challenges such as centralisation risks and technical limitations, AI agents are expected to transform Web3 with early applications such as staking, leading the way. However, although regulatory and privacy concerns remain, the potential for innovation in the decentralised space is significant. Coindesk [believes Web3 gaming is set to break out in 2025](https://www.coindesk.com/opinion/2024/12/16/how-web3-consumer-apps-will-finally-break-out-in-2025-6-predictions) with AI playing a key role in [non-player characters](https://www.tomorrowsworldtoday.com/artificial-intelligence/what-are-non-player-characters-in-video-games/) (NPCs) and autonomous interactive worlds. Established gaming studios are also integrating blockchain for decentralised economies and player ownership - meme culture will continue driving Web3 community engagement. Moreover, in-game marketplaces will evolve into sovereign economies with DeFi integration and advances in liquidity solutions will reduce fragmentation, whilst social utility-driven Web3 assets gain popularity. Notably, regulatory changes post-election may foster a more crypto-friendly environment, whereby supporting Web3 gaming's growth.

Back in 2021, Rtfkt, the Web3 streetwear brand was acquired by Nike but then [ceased operations in January 2025](https://www.voguebusiness.com/story/technology/rtfkt-andnbspan-early-web3-fashion-success-story-is-folding#:~:text=Rtfkt%20%E2%80%94%20the%20Web3%20streetwear%20brand,new%20chapter%E2%80%9D%20for%20the%20company.). Known for blending sneaker culture, gaming and NFTs, the brand succeeded in achieving $185.3 million in NFT sales and partnered itself with names such as Takashi Murakami and Ledger; celebrating early success though, it unfortunately struggled to sustain revenue amidst the broader NFT market downturn. Yet, despite this, Animoca Brands chairman, Yat Siu, confidently anticipates that non-fungible tokens (NFTs) will still surpass their 2021 peak performance in 2025. In an interview at the Bitcoin MENA event in Abu Dhabi, Siu expressed with optimism that NFTs will become _“even bigger”_ than during 2021 and 2022 (when monthly volumes ranged from $1 billion to $5 billion and peaked at $6 billion in January 2022). So, it seems 2025 has its job marked out for NFTs, and that is, to decide their future. Web3 is not simply about decentralisation anymore - it is actually about survival. If this next generation of the internet can overcome liquidity fragmentation, regulatory crackdowns and user adoption hurdles, it could not only redefine the internet but also finance and ownership. If not, 2025 could mark the beginning of Web3’s disillusionment phase.

The next 12 months will no doubt enlighten us as to whether Web3 is an unstoppable force, or merely another failed experiment in digital idealism. Web3 stands at a crossroads - either cementing itself as the backbone of the next digital era or facing a reckoning from regulators and market downturns. However, if the industry successfully navigates liquidity challenges, institutional adoption and AI-driven efficiencies, 2025 could be the turning point where Web3 evolves from a speculative trend to an indispensable pillar of the global economy.
