# How companies can manage their cash deposits better

*By Jonny Fry | *

Efficient cash management remains a major challenge for businesses today. Studies indicate that [80% of corporate operating cash](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2025/01/235542-fintech-ramp-launches-treasury-product-for-cash-management-that-enhances-business-operations/) sits in bank accounts, earning little to no interest. In the UK alone, businesses may be foregoing £992 million in interest annually by holding [£103.5 billion in low-yield deposit accounts](https://www.utbank.co.uk/savings-and-deposits/uk-sme-businesses-losing-out-deposit-interest/). Yet, with more than [400 million SMEs worldwide](https://globalnaps.org/issue/small-medium-enterprises-smes/#:~:text=The%20approximately%20400%20million%20SMEs,Responsible%20Business%20Conduct%20in%20Georgia%5D.), optimising cash management could unlock substantial extra revenue. There are a variety of digital-powered solutions already being used to help companies earn more from their money as well as making the process of payments faster and cheaper. Indeed, we are already seeing examples of companies using digital money to make payments. [PayPal, EY and Google Cloud use Coinbase for B2B payments](https://community.sap.com/t5/financial-management-blogs-by-sap/payments-without-boundaries-embark-on-the-future-of-payments-with-sap/ba-p/13875432) \- further evidence of stablecoins being used for payments. PayPal has successfully paid EY invoices using PayPal USD (PYUSD) and, as [Steve Everett](https://www.coinbase.com/blog/paypal-ey-and-google-cloud-use-coinbase-for-b2b-payments), Director of Market Development at PayPal, explains: _“B2B payments is ripe for innovation - and digital currencies can provide incredible value in these use cases. Requiring terms like ‘net-30’ for invoice payments can restrict cash flow and negatively impact business operations. With digital currencies like stablecoins, payments can be made 24/7, funds transferred near instantly and settled in near real time - enabling businesses to put their money to work faster.”_

Many companies keep their operating cash in traditional business accounts that generate little to no interest, leading to significant financial inefficiencies and substantial lost earnings. Another example of new solutions is from Ramp, which is looking to offer businesses a way to [earn up to 35 times more](https://www.pymnts.com/news/b2b-payments/2025/ramp-adds-treasury-solution-to-financial-operations-platform/#:~:text=Ramp%20has%20added%20a%20treasury,said%20in%20a%20Wednesday%20\(Jan.) on its operating cash than the national average. Ramp’s approach enhances liquidity management by extending working capital by an average of three extra days, improving cash flow and allowing businesses to operate with greater financial flexibility. Additionally, the company optimises bill payments, ensuring suppliers are paid precisely when due to maximise liquidity without unnecessary early outflows. AI-powered cash forecasting and real-time alerts further strengthen liquidity management by providing businesses with better insights into their financial position and future cash needs. Certainly, with these innovations, competition in the UK business banking sector is intensifying as firms seek more efficient ways to maximise returns on their cash deposits. By investing in short-term, high-yield assets, businesses can boost income without affecting operational liquidity - automation further refines financial processes, integrating cash forecasting and facilitating seamless fund transfers to reduce manual effort and errors. **Tokenised money market funds (MMFs) - the future of business cash management**

Tokenised money market funds (MMFs) are poised to revolutionise business cash management, offering a more efficient alternative to traditional MMFs (which currently hold over [$9 trillion in assets globally](https://www.bis.org/publ/qtrpdf/r_qt2312d.htm#:~:text=With%20more%20than%20$9%20trillion,credit%20expansion%20by%20other%20NBFIs.)). Whilst corporate and institutional investors favour MMFs for their low-risk, short-term investment opportunities, conventional structures present challenges such as settlement delays of two to three business days and monthly interest payments that limit real-time earnings. Tokenised MMFs eliminate liquidity bottlenecks by providing businesses with round-the-clock access to funds, ensuring immediate capital deployment. Some platforms enable real-time interest payments, even accruing hourly, to maximise returns. Additionally, digital foreign exchange (FX) swap integration enhances cross-border liquidity management, simplifying and reducing the cost of international transactions. [CitiGroup](https://www.citigroup.com/global/news/press-release/2024/citi-and-fidelity-international-demonstrate-tokenized-money-market-fund-and-digital-foreign-exchange-swap-solution) highlights that combining tokenised MMFs with digital FX swaps could significantly improve liquidity management, allowing businesses to optimise their financial strategies while ensuring immediate cash accessibility. As fintech innovations continue, tokenised MMFs are gaining traction as a secure and efficient capital management solution.

**Stablecoins - rapid adoption in business finance**

Stablecoins are increasingly shaping corporate finance, particularly in international payments and commercial transactions. Circle’s USDC has emerged as a key player whereby experiencing rapid adoption. According to Circle’s annual report, USDC processed [$18 trillion in transactions last year, marking a 50% annual growth](https://www.circle.com/reports/state-of-the-usdc-economy). In November alone, USDC transactions hit $1 trillion whilst wallet holders rose by 45% to 3.9 million. And, although stablecoins were initially used for digital asset transactions, their expansion into real-world financial applications (especially global payments) is accelerating rapidly. Several key factors are driving this growth. Greater regulatory clarity has encouraged institutional adoption whilst scalable public blockchains have significantly improved transaction speeds. Additionally, enhanced accessibility through new B2B payment infrastructures, such as Stripe’s acquisition of Bridge, is making stablecoins more viable for corporate use. Circle’s extensive banking partnerships across the US, Brazil, Mexico and Asia have further reinforced its leadership in stablecoin off-ramping, with $850 million already converted between USDC and fiat currencies. Stablecoins are also making inroads into FX markets. Standard Chartered’s [Zodia Markets recently minted $4 billion in USDC](https://www.gate.io/learn/articles/the-dollar-on-the-value-internet-2025-usdc-market-economy-report/6035) to streamline settlements between the Asia-Pacific region and the US. And, whilst [USDC’s $1 trillion in transactions last November](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/usdc-stablecoin-reaches-1-trillion-monthly-transactions/) is still far below JP Morgan’s $10 trillion daily payment volume, it signals a major milestone in stablecoin adoption. Circle’s message is clear: stablecoins are only at the beginning of their journey, and their role in real-world business payments is set to expand rapidly.

**Programmable bank accounts - automating business finances**

A programmable bank account using much of the existing payment infrastructure could revolutionise corporate finance by combining high interest rates, real-time payments and automated financial operations. This innovative model would allow businesses to earn interest on deposits whilst maintaining instant access to funds, so creating a more dynamic and efficient cash management system. In addition, to ensure security and stability, these accounts would ideally be backed by high-quality assets such as central bank reserves or money market funds (MMFs). Beyond liquidity management, programmability introduces a new level of automation in financial transactions. Smart contracts could trigger payments 24/7, based on predefined conditions, so eliminating delays and inefficiencies. For instance, a supplier payment could be executed automatically once an IoT device confirms the delivery of goods. Similarly, stock trading payments could occur in real-time once settlement conditions are met, and invoice payments could be processed upon verification of contract milestones. By leveraging blockchain technology, programmable bank accounts could significantly enhance operational efficiency whilst reducing liquidity risk. Moreover, their integration with existing financial regulations ensures businesses could maintain full compliance whilst optimising cash flow. Certainly, as adoption grows, this shift toward programmable finance could redefine how businesses manage payments, investments and financial operations and provide regulators with tangible use cases of how programmable payments can be used.

Essentially,**** businesses today are losing billions by keeping operating cash in low-interest accounts whereby missing out on potential earnings and liquidity optimisation. However, innovative financial solutions are reshaping how companies manage their cash. High-yield business savings accounts provide better returns, whilst cash management platforms enable dynamic fund allocation. Tokenised money market funds offer real-time access to interest-bearing assets and stablecoin-powered payments enhance transaction speed and efficiency. Meanwhile, programmable bank accounts integrate automation, allowing businesses to execute payments and manage liquidity seamlessly. And, as digital finance continues to evolve, companies that adopt these new strategies will gain a competitive edge so ensuring greater financial agility and efficiency. The future of business cash management is programmable, automated and digital - firms that embrace this transformation will thrive in an increasingly fast-paced financial landscape.
