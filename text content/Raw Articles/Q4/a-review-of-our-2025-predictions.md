# A review of our 2025 predictions

*By Jonny Fry | *

As 2025 draws to a close, our thoughts begin to turn to 2026 and what it holds in store for us all. In next week’s Digital Bytes we will have a selection of thoughts and predictions for 2026, but this week we review what we had thought 2025 would offer.

In an effort to be dispassionate and independent, we asked three AI bots for their verdicts on our predictions.

> · **ChaptGPT** \- overall verdict and estimated accuracy: **80 - 85%**. Most core themes materialised, although timelines for CBDCs and full retail access (UK ETFs, energy tokenisation at scale) were more gradual than forecast.
> 
> · **Grok** -**** overall**** accuracy:**85%**. Tokenisation and institutional adoption surged, propelled by regulatory tailwinds, although US policy lagged on CBDCs. 15 of 18 core predictions materialised or exceeded benchmarks, with market sizes often surpassing estimates.
> 
> · **Gemini 3** \- overall 2025 prediction accuracy: **90%**. The collection of predictions regarding the blockchain and digital asset landscape for 2025 proved to be exceptionally accurate, capturing the major shifts that have defined the year. The core thesis of the predictions – (that the industry would transition from a niche, speculative space to a foundational, institutional infrastructure) was strongly validated by real-world events and data through late 2025.

**Therefore, according to our trustee AI bots: on a simplistic average, our 2025 predictions scored 85.9%**

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JZ4u!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e645066-00ef-485a-a664-308d7fea0cbc_224x160.png)

We asked the AI bot, Perplxity, regarding:

> · **Legal clarity and standards** \- MiCA has indeed created a much clearer, harmonised framework for issuers and service providers across the EU, especially for stablecoins and centralised crypto‑asset service providers. This is now a reference point for other jurisdictions and is laying the groundwork for more compliant tokenisation activity.
> 
> · **Investor protection** \- the regulation does strengthen disclosure, governance and conduct rules, so the claim that it _“enhances investor protections”_ is accurate.
> 
> · **Impact on tokenisation** \- MiCA indirectly supports tokenisation by clarifying how many crypto‑assets should be treated, but it is only one piece of the EU’s digital‑finance package (alongside DLT pilot regimes, securities law tweaks, etc.). Calling tokenisation _“poised for a significant transformation”_ is reasonable as a forward‑looking statement, yet by late 2025 most of the impact is still emerging rather than fly visible in volumes or market structure.
> 
> · **Smaller issuers and institutional participation** \- it is also fair to say MiCA imposes heavier compliance costs on smaller issuers while giving larger, regulated firms more confidence to engage, but evidence of materially improved secondary‑market liquidity or _“global scalability”_ is still limited at this stage.

This 2025 prediction, scores **7/10** : accurate on direction and regulatory content, somewhat optimistic on the speed and scale of the tokenisation _“transformation”_ that is already under way.
