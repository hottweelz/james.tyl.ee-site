# From Bitcoin to balance sheets: how corporates are winning crypto’s treasury

*By Jonny Fry | *

As digital assets enter corporate balance sheets and decentralised treasuries scale, the contest between decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs), companies and hybrid models is shaping a new financial order. Whilst DAOs emphasise transparency and community control, corporate treasuries apply traditional financial discipline.[ In addition, foundations and hybrid systems](https://cryptotale.org/who-leads-in-2025s-crypto-treasury-model-dao-or-corporate/) blend both approaches to improve efficiency and stability. But the defining question of 2025 remains: which model leads in the management of on-chain wealth? [Corporate Bitcoin treasuries](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/corporate-bitcoin-holdings-surge-40-091057750.html) control the market in institutional growth and realised outcomes, with [Bitwise reporting in Q3 2025](https://www.mexc.com/en-NG/news/corporate-bitcoin-ownership-jumped-38-in-q3-2025/130417) that 172 publicly traded companies currently own Bitcoin - an increase of 38% in a single quarter. Of note, when combined, their holdings exceed one million BTC (valued at [approximately $117 billion).](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/crypto-market-update-corporate-bitcoin-treasuries-jump-us-117-billion-0?utm)

[Strategy Inc. (formerly MicroStrategy)](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/MARA/) continues to be the largest holder of Bitcoin with 640,250 BTC, followed by MARA Holdings with 53,250 BTC; the introduction of[ fair-value accounting standards](https://www.blockhead.co/2025/10/31/strategy-reports-2-8-billion-profit-as-bitcoin-holdings-surge-past-640-000-btc/) has strengthened balance sheets by allowing unrealised Bitcoin gains to appear in earnings. Strategy’s $9.97 billion profit in Q2 2025 highlights the financial potential of these treasuries. Indeed, Tether recently purchased [over $90million of Bitcoin](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/tether-buys-dip-97m-bitcoin-121831272.html) and is the sixth largest holder of the cryptocurrency (valued at $8.4billion), sitting on over $4.55billion of unrealised profits. The expansion as perceived by institutional analysts is an indication of confidence that will be permanent. CoinDesk has reported that the amount of accumulation of corporations is [1,755 BTC, which is almost twice that which is mined](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2025/08/30/businesses-are-absorbing-bitcoin-at-4x-the-rate-it-is-mined-according-to-river-s-research) \- the majority of acquisitions are done via over-the-counter markets which restricts volatility of pricing but constrains the supply in the long run. However, the plan is not limited to Bitcoin - other corporations are adding Ether and Solana to diversify and match the ecosystem growth. By the beginning of October, [Bitcoin ETFs](https://financefeeds.com/bitwise-reports-48-new-bitcoin-treasuries-added/) had inflows totalling 2.7 billion dollars, an indication of a high demand by traditional investors who strive to have regulated exposure on digital assets. Furthermore, corporate models, which are supported by the governance and compliance frameworks, are effective in the allocation of capital and risk management and [DAOs remain the centre of on-chain finance](https://www.bobsguide.com/decentralized-autonomous-organizations-reshaping-governance/?), controlling $billions of assets in various ecosystems. The most important DAOs include Lido, Aave, Eigen and Uniswap, with total value locked (TVL) [of $30 billion, $33billion, $14billion and $4.9 billion](https://defillama.com/protocols) respectively.

Their activities are fully open, and the financial flows can be audited in real time. Nonetheless, there is a great diversity in terms of diversification and quality of governance in DAO treasuries. Most of them are still too much focused in native tokens which expose them to market volatility. Uniswap has a [Treasury Working Group ](https://gov.uniswap.org/t/uniswap-treasury-working-group-utwg-application/23571)to consider diversification; the Uniswap Foundation currently has stable reserves totalling $53.4 million, not including the larger token-based treasury. Meanwhile, [Arbitrum](https://www.theblock.co/post/353631/arbitrum-dao-us-treasurys) and Optimism are layer-2 DAOs that show alternative approaches to management. The diversification of the treasury of Arbitrum in [tokenised US treasuries](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/arbitrumdao-selects-franklin-templeton-spiko-and-wisdomtree-for-step-2-to-expand-rwa-adoption-on-chain-302449513.html) is an indicator of transition to low risk yield products. By contrast, Optimism, raises sequencer revenue to public goods funding whereby improving ecosystem development. Yet, in spite of it, low voter turnout and slow decision-making in DAOs persist; the processes of governance usually take a long time to reach a consensus, and this makes them less responsive in volatile markets. However, their transparent, collective structure remains unmatched for community engagement and accountability.

**Foundations and hybrid treasuries bridge two worlds**

Blockchain foundations now lead in structured, policy-driven financial management. In June 2025, the [Ethereum Foundation (EF) established a new policy](https://cryptonews.com.au/news/ethereum-foundation-announces-new-treasury-policy-for-critical-2025-26-period-129348/), the Treasury Policy, which limits its yearly expenditures and maintains a 2.5-year operating buffer - it also allows the generation of yields using audited[ DeFi systems](https://blog.ethereum.org/2025/06/04/ef-treasury-policy). This method balances sustainability in the long term with transparency and will guarantee certain predictable income towards the growth of the ecosystem. The [Solana Foundation](https://www.theblock.co/post/374029/solana-foundations-many-discounted-token-deals-fuels-sol-treasury-explosion) has a different but equally strategic direction. It has implemented some token placements with infrastructure partners where it has raised up $50 million on a vesting basis. This money was used to fund the growth of validators and network emergency response but the campaign received criticism due to the concentration risks. The example of Polkadot and Cosmos Hub hybrid[ treasury models ](https://supra.com/academy/polkadot-vs-cosmos/)is an integration of institutional discipline and on-chain governance. Polkadot’s OpenGov process manages a $135 million treasury through public referenda and working groups, and Cosmos Hub finances its activities with a staking tax and yield-generation schemes such as Hydro. Both systems are fully transparent based on auditable reports and real-time dashboards. In essence, these hybrid systems represent the new balance between decentralisation and control - they maintain community engagement with transparent financial strategies, a pattern that is becoming more adopted in large blockchain economies.

Meanwhile, [asset tokenisation in the real world (RWA)](https://www.thecoinrepublic.com/2025/10/15/blackrock-ceo-pushes-tokenization-as-rwa-market-hits-34-billion-all-time-high/) has become primary to contemporary treasury management. With the help of the transformation of assets such as[ US Treasury bonds into digital tokens](https://www.chainup.com/blog/tokenized-treasury-bonds-private-credit-blockchain-finance/), the treasuries receive the real-time settlement and minimise counterparty risk. Smart contracts automatise payment of yield, allowing 24/7 and borderless financial transactions. And, as of October 2025, the number of [tokenised assets had passed $33.91 billion](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/every-asset-tokenized-5-years-195039894.html), with US Treasuries making up $8.3 billion dollars.[ Notably, BlackRock BUIDL](https://www.ccn.com/education/crypto/blackrock-buidl-fund-tokenized-money-markets-explained/?) fund dominates this market with a market share of 15% and manages $2.82 billion dollars. This is the next wave of opportunity, with tokenisation (as described by CEO Larry Fink) forecasting the connection between traditional finance and on-chain infrastructure. T-bills are tokenised and gaining more and more stablecoin reserves in DAO and foundation treasuries, which are a combination of security and liquidity. This is a transition to hybrid yield strategies with less exposure to highly volatile DeFi markets and transparency. Treasury models are converging as corporations are testing staking and tokenised yields, and foundations are implementing on-chain dashboards to achieve transparency. DAOs are also implementing budgetary controls and audits in order that they can become institutionalised. This trend is being strengthened by macro conditions including increased inflows into ETFs, loosening monetary policy and faster tokenisation of assets. The resultant outcome is a growing ecosystem that is a hybrid of [crypto-native agility](https://www.soprasteria.com/insights/details/traditional-finance-and-crypto-hybridisation-in-motion?) and institutional governance rigor.

Seemingly therefore, in 2025, the balance of power in crypto finance is shifting from code to corporations. Once the preserve of decentralised communities, digital treasuries are now being institutionalised as publicly quoted companies such as Strategy Inc. and others holding BTC as opposed to US Dollars. The disciplined approach of corporates contrasts with DAOs’ slower, more democratic governance and with blockchain foundations that blend transparency with policy control. Meanwhile, the rise of tokenised treasuries, now exceeding $33billion, signals a merging of traditional finance and DeFi. Hence, the future of crypto treasuries may not lie with decentralisation alone but with hybrid models marrying corporate structure and on-chain accountability.
