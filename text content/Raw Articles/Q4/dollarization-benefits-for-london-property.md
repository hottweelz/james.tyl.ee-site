# Dollarization benefits for London property

*By Jonny Fry | *

**Benefits for UK monetary and fiscal policies**

If US dollar stablecoins (digital currencies pegged 1:1 to the US dollar, such as USDT or USDC) were deployed as the primary denomination for prime real estate transactions in London, it would represent a form of partial ‘dollarization’ in a high-value asset sector. Prime residential London properties (e.g., luxury homes in Mayfair, Kensington, etc, often valued at £5-50 million) attract significant foreign investment, particularly from dollar rich Middle Eastern, Asian and US buyers who are seeking direct exposure to the asset class. As the chart below indicates, London continues to be one of the most favoured cities globally. Indeed, according to[ Knight Frank:](https://www.knightfrank.com/research/article/2023-12-15-after-the-pandemic-london-superprime-market-rebounds-as-country-house-demand-cools?utm_source=perplexity&utm_medium=ai&utm_campaign=perplexity_referral) “ _The number of individual property sales above £10 million in the capital has hit an eight-year high._ ” This shift could accelerate significantly with the new regulatory tailwinds such as the US GENIUS Act of 2025, which aggressively promotes global USD stablecoin adoption, potentially pressuring the UK to adapt to direct USD stablecoin use in the domestic economy. Based on analyses from the Bank of England (BoE) and other sources, the primary effects of this would challenge UK monetary and fiscal sovereignty over its currency and economy, while ancillary impacts ripple through the real estate ecosystem.

**Monetary policy (Bank of England)**

The BoE’s mandate focuses on maintaining inflation at 2% and supporting financial stability through tools like interest rates, quantitative easing and reserve requirements. A shift to US dollar stablecoins for prime real estate could undermine these by:

> · **reducing control over money supply and transmission**

Transactions in USD stablecoins bypass the GBP system, reducing demand for sterling-denominated loans and deposits. [The BoE’s July 2025 Financial Stability Report ](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/financial-stability-report/2025/july-2025)highlights that broader stablecoin use could fragment the payments landscape, weakening monetary policy transmission as interest rate changes affect GBP less directly. This echoes the [Bank of International Settlement’s](https://www.bis.org/publ/arpdf/ar2025e3.htm) concerns that stablecoins could divert deposits from UK banks, shrinking the BoE’s balance sheet and limiting tools such as QE. In a dollarized real estate market, capital inflows might stabilize during GBP weakness but exacerbate volatility if investors flee to US dollars during crises.

> · **adding to exchange rate and inflation pressures**

Increased US dollar stablecoin usage could weaken GBP as real estate deals (worth ~£50–100 billion annually in prime London) shift to dollar-pegged assets. This might import US inflation or Fed policy effects, complicating the effectiveness of BoE targeting. A [2025 McKinsey report ](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/the-stable-door-opens-how-tokenized-cash-enables-next-gen-payments)notes stablecoins’ role in cross-border payments could amplify this, with dollar-denominated transactions rising to $trillions globally by 2030. The BoE might respond by raising rates to defend GBP, risking even slower (or even negative) growth.

> · **increasing financial stability risks**

The report warns of stablecoin runs (e.g., de-pegging events such as USDC in 2023) spilling into UK markets, especially if real estate relies on them. Partial dollarization could erode seigniorage (profits from issuing GBP), forcing the BoE to innovate, such as revisiting or accelerating CBDC development (e.g., digital pound pilots).Overall, this would dilute BoE autonomy, potentially leading to tighter regulations under the FCA’s CP25/14 framework, which proposes oversight for stablecoin issuance to mitigate systemic risks.

**Fiscal policy (UK Treasury)**

The Treasury manages taxation, spending and borrowing and, according to the [Office for Budget Responsibility:](https://obr.uk/forecasts-in-depth/tax-by-tax-spend-by-spend/property-transaction-taxes/) “ _In 2025-26 we estimate that property transaction taxes will raise £15.7 billion. This represents 1.3 per cent of all receipts and is equivalent to around £540 per household and 0.5 per cent of national income. US Dollar stablecoin dominance would complicate this revenue and expenditure._ ” Tax collection challenges include:

> · **properties denominated in US dollar stablecoins** -**** could evade GBP-based taxes if transactions occur on blockchains outside UK jurisdiction. The Treasury might face shortfalls, as stamp duty ([up to 12% on high-value properties](https://www.kfh.co.uk/buyers/stamp-duty-calculator/#:~:text=The%20stamp%20duty%20rate%20ranges,are%20a%20multiple%20home%20owner.)) is levied in GBP. Citibank suggests stablecoins could expand to be in excess of [$3.7 trillion by 2030,](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/citi-stablecoins-could-reach-3-7-trillion-by-2030-in-bull-scenario/) enabling offshore evasion and pressuring fiscal balances. Responses could include crypto-specific taxes or requiring GBP conversion for settlements.
> 
> · **increased foreign investment and revenue potential** \- attracting USD-holding investors (e.g., via lower volatility) could significantly boost property values and indirect tax revenues (e.g., VAT on services). However, dollarization might also widen inequality, as locals face higher prices in a GBP-weakened market, prompting Treasury subsidies or affordable housing initiatives.
> 
> · **borrowing and debt dynamics** -**** greater US dollar inflows could ease gilt issuance but expose the Treasury to FX risks if revenues fluctuate with GBP-USD rates. The 2025 BIS Annual Report notes stablecoins’ potential to disrupt fiscal-monetary coordination, possibly requiring new borrowing in stablecoins.

The Treasury might adapt via international cooperation, aligning with EU stances on dollar stablecoins’ monetary impacts.

**Ancillary effects on real estate ecosystem**

> · **estate agents**

[Barclays’ private bank](https://privatebank.barclays.com/insights/londons-prime-property-market-the-show-goes-on-02-2025/) reported _: “Demand from American buyers especially has been building steadily over the past 12 to 24 months, and they now make up nearly 30% of our clients,” says Eccles at Eccord. Currency discounts, along with the appeal of the UK lifestyle and education system, remain strong drivers. One of our super-prime American clients told us he had considered his options globally but concluded, “There’s only one London”, and is now buying a £20 million house here.”_ How long before leading real estate agents such as Christies, Savills, Knight Frank, CBRE, etc, pivot to selling property using digital payments. Benefits include faster international deals (e.g., 24/7 near atomic settlements via blockchain), attracting [20–30% more global buyers](https://www.remitone.com/saudi-arabia-and-the-gcc-how-to-tap-into-one-of-the-worlds-biggest-payments-markets-ipr-global-2023/#:~:text=This%20strategic%20approach%20has%20the%20potential%20to,utilisation%20of%20it%20in%20the%20current%20market.). The drawbacks? Training costs for wallet management, AML compliance under FCA rules and volatility risks if stablecoins de-peg. Commissions might rise 0.5-1% for crypto handling, but competition from fintech agents could erode margins.

> · **marketing of properties**

Marketing would quickly globalize, with listings on platforms such as Rightmove or Zoopla increasingly emphasizing US dollar stablecoin pricing to appeal to non-GBP investors. Matt Green, partner at Stephens Lawrence, confirmed its real estate team is already handling property transactions using digital assets. Furthermore, [a 2025 Bird & Bird](https://www.twobirds.com/en/insights/2025/uk/stablecoin-acceptance-and-the-future-of-the-uk-stablecoin-sector) report notes growing stablecoin and non-fiat acceptance in payments, enabling VR (virtual reality) tours and token-based viewings for foreign investors. Increased digital ads targeting stablecoin or crypto holders, potentially boosting marketing budgets 15–20%, but requiring FX hedging disclosures to comply with consumer protection laws.

> · **conveyancers and solicitors**

These professionals manage title transfers and due diligence. According to a report in 2025 from[ McKinesy](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/the-stable-door-opens-how-tokenized-cash-enables-next-gen-payments), stablecoins introduce smart contracts for automated escrows, reducing transaction timelines from 12-16 weeks to days or potentially hours. The challenges include legal ambiguities in blockchain titles under UK land registry (HM Land Registry pilots digital deeds since 2024), heightened KYC/AML scrutiny and disputes over stablecoin valuation. Fees might increase when making payments using digital payments expertise, with solicitors advising on tax implications, potentially spurring specialization in “digital asset-conveyancing”. The implications are stark. For the BoE, stablecoin settlements bypass GBP deposits, weakening monetary policy transmission and potentially importing US interest rate and inflation dynamics. For the Treasury, property tax collection becomes more complex if deals are executed on offshore blockchains, whilst FX volatility could shake revenues and debt planning. And for the market itself, estate agents, marketers and conveyancers may embrace faster, 24/7 cross-border deals, even as they face new AML, KYC, and de-pegging risks.

Partial dollarization of London real estate wouldn’t just be a quirk of the crypto age; it could erode UK monetary sovereignty in its most elite economic enclave, whilst entrenching the dollar’s role as the true global reserve currency - one luxury penthouse at a time. However, is dollarization of prime real estate ultimately what the UK government had in mind when they agreed to set up a [task force with the US](https://fintechmagazine.com/news/markets-of-the-future-uk-us-establish-taskforce) to look at closer ties around capital markets and digital assets..?
