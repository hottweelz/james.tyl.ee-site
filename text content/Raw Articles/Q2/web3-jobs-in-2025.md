# Web3 jobs in 2025

*By Jonny Fry | *

In the early days of Web3, it felt like a frontier town with a few core roles such as blockchain developers and crypto traders leading the charge. Fast forward to today, and that frontier has blossomed into a sprawling ecosystem! The sheer growth and diversification of the Web3 ecosystem have given birth to a dazzling array of new and specialised roles, many of which did not even exist a few short years ago. From moderators of decentralised communities to architects of immersive metaverse experiences and strategists navigating the complexities of tokenomics, the career landscape within Web3 is now as expansive and dynamic as the technology itself. Here are some recent roles and what they entail:

> · **regional Web3 researcher** \- conducts in-depth analysis of the Web3 landscape within a specific geographic region to identify opportunities and challenges. This involves market and competitive analysis, monitoring local regulatory developments, identifying regional trends, building local networks and producing insightful reports. Essential skills include market analysis, competitive analysis, regulatory monitoring, trend identification and research reporting.
> 
> · **proof of humanity lead:** steers the growth and maintenance of [the Proof of Humanity Sybil-resistance system](https://docs.kleros.io/products/proof-of-humanity), which aims to ascertain that a user is a real human and not a bot attacker. This includes managing the community, participating in governance and protocol development, implementing strategies to combat Sybil attacks, overseeing the verification process and fostering partnerships. It requires strong skills in community management, governance participation, a deep understanding of Sybil resistance techniques and blockchain technology.
> 
> · **flash loan arbitrageur** \- identifies and executes arbitrage opportunities in DeFi using flash loans. This involves monitoring price discrepancies across DEXs, developing and deploying automated trading bots, understanding DeFi protocols and managing the inherent risks associated with flash loan transactions.**** Critical skills include a deep understanding of DeFi protocols, the ability to identify arbitrage opportunities, basic smart contract knowledge for bot interaction and strong risk management abilities.
> 
> · **Web3 data labels researcher** \- collects, cleans and labels diverse Web3 datasets to support the training of machine learning models. This involves identifying relevant data sources, developing labelling guidelines, performing accurate data annotation and ensuring data quality.**** Essential skills required include a foundational understanding of Web3 concepts, strong data literacy, meticulous attention to detail and proficiency in data labelling techniques.
> 
> · **investigator** \- conducts thorough investigations into security breaches, scams, fraud and other illicit activities within the Web3 ecosystem. This involves analysing blockchain transactions, tracing cryptocurrency flows, examining smart contract vulnerabilities, gathering digital evidence and potentially collaborating with law enforcement or other relevant authorities.**** Requires strong analytical and investigative skills, a deep understanding of blockchain technology and cryptocurrency transactions, proficiency in using blockchain explorers and forensic tools and the ability to synthesise complex information into clear findings.

These emerging roles signal a broader transformation where Web3 is moving from experimental to foundational, requiring interdisciplinary talent to build systems that are not only decentralised but also practical, humane and scalable. Roles such as DAO strategists, protocol relations managers and community economists highlight the shift towards sustainable governance, interoperability and incentive design. Zero-knowledge engineers and smart contract auditors are becoming essential as privacy, scalability and security take centre stage.

If you have the skills required for a role, several specialised job platforms offer a range of opportunities - [CryptoJobsList](https://cryptojobslist.com/) is renowned for its extensive daily listings across various roles and skill sets; [BitcoinJobs.com](http://bitcoinjobs.com) caters specifically to positions within Bitcoin-focused organisations; [Crypto.Jobs](http://crypto.jobs) connects companies with top crypto talent globally; [CryptocurrencyJobs.co](https://cryptocurrencyjobs.co/) emphasises high-level technical positions suitable for engineers and senior tech professionals; [Crypto-Careers.com](http://crypto-careers.com) focuses on competitive tech roles from major exchanges and venture firms; platforms such as [Wellfound](https://wellfound.com/) (formerly AngelList) and [Dream Startup Job](https://www.dreamstartupjob.com/) provide startup-centric opportunities; and for freelancers, [UpWork](https://www.upwork.com/) offers flexible projects in the crypto and blockchain domains. Meanwhile, if you do not have the skills, there are other ways to catch up. To fill the roles, many professionals are reskilling and upskilling: rather than traditional degrees, individuals are turning to more agile, community-driven learning models such as online certifications, bootcamps and learning DAOs. Platforms such as [ChainShot](https://www.alchemy.com/dapps/chainshot), [Encode Club](https://www.encode.club/) and Web3 University offer intensive coding bootcamps focused on smart contract development and blockchain architecture. Learning DAOs such as [Developer DAO](https://www.developerdao.com/) and [Buildspace](https://buildspace.so/) provide immersive, hands-on experiences where participants can contribute to real projects while learning in public. Similarly, certifications from platforms such as [ConsenSys Academy](https://courses.consensys.net/) or [Alchemy University](https://www.alchemy.com/university) are gaining recognition as valid credentials.

Furthermore, Web3 has always prioritised proof-of-work, collaboration and continuous learning over formal credentials, whereby enabling a new wave of self-taught builders, strategists and creatives to enter the space from diverse backgrounds. But whilst the industry offers attractive benefits such as remote work and competitive salaries, it also presents significant legal risks, particularly due to its decentralised nature and varying international regulations. It advises job seekers to thoroughly assess potential employers, understand regional cryptocurrency laws and be cautious of projects that may operate in legal grey areas. This can be as literal as Web3 firms being [warned by the US, Japan and South Korea](https://www.binance.com/en-NG/square/post/18925151454274) as regards hiring from North Korea due to its ties to the infamous [Lazarus group](https://www.nccgroup.com/uk/the-lazarus-group-north-korean-scourge-for-plus10-years/). Priority surely ought to be placed on evaluating a project's vision, technical solutions and the credibility of its team members. In this way, job seekers get to avoid falling into the hands of those [hackers](https://decrypt.co/308027/crazy-evil-hackers-create-fake-web3-firm-drain-job-seekers-crypto-wallets) and [scammers](https://www.ccn.com/education/crypto/fake-crypto-job-scams-spot-avoid-web3-fraud/) who are waiting to steal and defraud job seekers. So, as much as Web3 is the land of opportunities, to malicious actors, the opportunities are ripe in unsuspecting people. Additionally, seekers are [urged to avoid roles](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/20724184829281) that could inadvertently involve them in illegal activities, such as money laundering or unlicensed financial operations with corporate bodies. If a corporate job does not appeal, you could tread the self-employed path and create roles for yourself - as did [Yellow Panther](https://www.yellowpanther.io/). The 26-year-old is a prominent Web3 gamer who transitioned from competitive gaming to advising major gaming companies and creating content for a substantial online following. He [explains that full-time gaming](https://cointelegraph.com/magazine/yellow-panther-gaming-job-ai-agent-crypto-game-immutable-web3-gamer/) involves managing various business operations beyond simply playing.

So, as the space matures, it is not merely about building the technology but also about ensuring its responsible and sustainable integration into society - such as how Yellow Panther brought Web3 to Web2 through content creation and influencing. Whether pursuing a traditional corporate path or forging an independent career, professionals in Web3 must navigate a complex environment with a focus on continuous learning, adaptability and a commitment to shaping the future of the decentralised web. The message? Know who you are working for - **stay legal and stay smart.**
