# How SPACs are being deployed for digital assets: 21 Capital

*By Jonny Fry | *

A roster of Web3 companies have been attempting to go public for some time now. [Circle, Kraken, Gemini, Ripple and Bullish](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/markets/5-crypto-firms-eyeing-ipos-include-circle-kraken-ripple/#:~:text=its%20IPO%20plans.-,Gemini,sources%20told%20Bloomberg%20on%20Thursday.) are reconsidering IPO plans amid a more favourable US regulatory environment under President Trump. Circle has an active SEC filing and has [moved headquarters to New York](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/2024-09-14-circle-relocates-global-headquarters-to-new-york-city-13525214520842); Kraken is weighing a public offering but has only [raised a fraction](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2024/06/06/kraken-said-to-be-raising-over-100m-pre-ipo-funding-bloomberg) of its targeted pre-IPO funding; Gemini is reviving IPO plans after market turmoil stalled earlier efforts; Ripple may move forward once its SEC legal battle wraps up; and Bullish, after shelving Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC) plans, is now preparing for a traditional IPO.

Alongside this wave, a new entrant has emerged - 21 Capital (backed by Tether, Bitfinex and SoftBank) is under the leadership of Cantor Fitzgerald’s, Brandon Lutnick and is positioning itself to reshape how Bitcoin-focused firms access public markets. 21 will operate through a SPAC, a model that allows firms to bypass the long and expensive traditional IPO process. Instead of issuing new shares through an underwriter, a private company merges with an already-listed SPAC, immediately becoming public (SPACs offer an alternative route for companies to go public).The venture/merger, operating under Cantor Equity Partners, which [raised $200 million in January 2025](https://www.cantor.com/tether-softbank-group-and-jack-mallers-launch-twenty-one-a-bitcoin-native-company-through-a-business-combination-with-cantor-equity-partners/), will receive $3 billion in Bitcoin contributions: $1.5 billion from Tether, $900 million from SoftBank and $600 million from Bitfinex, and the merger will reshape the firm's ownership. 21 Capital will be majority-owned by Tether and Bitfinex, with Tether holding a 43% stake and Bitfinex holding a 16% stake. SoftBank will hold a minority stake of 24%, and Cantor affiliates will hold 1.9%. Existing SPAC shareholders will be heavily diluted, retaining a mere 2.7% of the total shares. Lutnick’s SPAC will also [raise an additional $585 million](https://www.ft.com/content/501210ad-d39b-4d7b-b649-bbd08ceffe6f), split between a $385 million convertible bond and a $200 million private placement, both intended to purchase more Bitcoin. Following this, Tether, Bitfinex and SoftBank will convert their Bitcoin investments into shares priced at $13 for the private placement and $10 for the bond, valuing Bitcoin at a striking $85,000 per coin. This strategy closely mirrors MicroStrategy’s approach of buying and stockpiling Bitcoin to increase shareholder value.

At launch, [21 Capital plans to hold over 42,000 Bitcoin](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250423962305/en/Tether-SoftBank-Group-and-Jack-Mallers-Launch-Twenty-One-a-Bitcoin-native-Company-Through-a-Business-Combination-With-Cantor-Equity-Partners), making it the third-largest corporate Bitcoin holder, behind MicroStrategy’s 538,200 and Marathon Holdings’ 47,531. And whilst there is still a long way to go before matching MicroStrategy’s dominance, Twenty One is modifying the playbook in its own way. The publication, BeinCrypto, [reports](https://beincrypto.com/max-keiser-bitcoin-forecast-2-2m-21-capital-strategy-showdown/) that Steven Lubka, Head of Swan Private Wealth, believes 21 Capital could rival Saylor’s MicroStrategy. Jack Mallers is promoting Bitcoin-native metrics, such as Bitcoin Per Share (BPS), directly challenging MicroStrategy’s model of offering indirect Bitcoin exposure through MSTR stock. Still, Lubka [notes that this unintended competition](https://x.com/DzambhalaHODL/status/1915051486717022664) could ultimately benefit MicroStrategy, potentially increasing its value. Max Keiser also [pointed out](https://beincrypto.com/max-keiser-bitcoin-forecast-2-2m-21-capital-strategy-showdown/) the likelihood of a “showdown” between the two entities and [believes](https://beincrypto.com/max-keiser-bitcoin-forecast-2-2m-21-capital-strategy-showdown/) that _“institutional Bitcoin FOMO will reach a 'fever pitch'.”_ However, Jack Mallers, joining Cantor Fitzgerald as co-founder and CEO of 21 Capital, has other ideas, stating: _"We're not here to beat the market, we're here to build a new one. A public stock, built by Bitcoiners, for Bitcoiners."_ With his experience as the CEO of Strike (a Bitcoin payment platform), he maintains that the new role would not distract him from his previous one. And, since both roles are Bitcoin-oriented, there is no telling if a sale or partnership could happen in the future, especially as the Lutnicks at Cantor have been making headway into Web3. Meanwhile, Cantor Fitzgerald’s launch of a new Bitcoin acquisition vehicle underscores its accelerating push into the digital asset sector. The firm has maintained close ties with Tether, previously advising on a [$775 million](https://tether.io/news/tether-announces-775-million-strategic-investment-in-rumble-to-boost-decentralized-and-community-owned-media-platforms/#:~:text=Tether%20Announces%20$775%20Million%20Strategic,controlling%20stake%20in%20the%20Company.) investment into video-sharing platform, Rumble, and backing the development of a proposed [$2 billion Bitcoin-collateralised lending program](https://fortune.com/crypto/2024/11/24/howard-lutnick-cantor-fitzgerald-tether-stablecoin-2-billion-bitcoin-lending/#:~:text=Funding%20for%20the%20program%20will,of%20its%20dominant%20USDT%20stablecoin.). Their relationship is longstanding - under Howard Lutnick’s leadership (before he was made the US Secretary of Commerce and appointed his son, Brandon Lutnick, CEO), Cantor emerged as a critical financial partner for Tether whereby safeguarding a significant portion of the dollar-denominated reserves underpinning its stablecoin. [And, according to Tether CEO, Paolo Ardoino,](https://www.reuters.com/technology/tether-is-talks-with-big-four-firm-about-reserve-audit-ceo-says-2025-03-21/) 99% of the US Treasury bills supporting Tether’s reserves are now custodied with Cantor.

In parallel, Cantor has established two additional SPACs under Brandon Lutnick - both aggressively seeking acquisitions in digital assets and fintech. The brokerage is also expanding its market infrastructure by adopting custody platforms from [Anchorage Digital](https://www.anchorage.com/insights/cantor-fitzgerald-selects-anchorage-digital-as-custodian-collateral-manager) and [Copper](https://copper.co/en/insights/company-news/cantor-fitzgerald-and-copper-partner-for-secure-bitcoin-financing), so signalling an increasingly sophisticated approach to crypto finance. Cantor Fitzgerald’s$3.6 Twenty-Firstcoin vehicle, 21 Capital, has redrawn the map of modern finance, but it also brings deep tensions. Firstly, Tether and Bitfinex’s 2021 settlements with New York regulators and the CFTC did not fully erase doubts about reserve transparency or shifting regulatory scrutiny. Secondly, Cantor Fitzgerald’s leadership, with Howard Lutnick moving into a government role whilst his son chairs the brokerage, blurs the lines between public duty and private gain, mirroring the [Trump family’s own SPAC and memecoin ventures](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/trump-family-crypto-dwf-1235318209/). Thirdly, the debate over Bitcoin as digital gold versus the dollar as transactional backbone highlights conflicting visions: can stablecoins and BTC co-exist? Fourthly, the rush to crown Bitcoin king risks repeating FOMO-driven mistakes, as seen in meme-token meltdowns and volatile price swings. Finally, the Trump clan’s Web3 ambitions underscore how political power and profit motives can entwine, raising fresh ethical concerns.

Essentially, 21 Capital presents an innovative investment vehicle for those seeking exposure to Bitcoin through traditional financial markets. One can invest in Bitcoin directly, invest in MicroStrategy - another US listed public company whose assets are predominately Bitcoin - buy Bitcoin mining stocks such as [Marathon, Riot Blockchain, Core Scientific](https://companiesmarketcap.com/gbp/bitcoin-mining/largest-bitcoin-mining-companies-by-marketcap/) etc, or buy a Bitcoin [ETF.](https://etfdb.com/themes/bitcoin-etfs/) However, potential investors should carefully consider the associated risks, including market volatility and regulatory uncertainties. Whose interests will prevail? Will it be Bitcoin? The dollar? Or an ecosystem where both can thrive under real scrutiny and balance? Time shall tell….
