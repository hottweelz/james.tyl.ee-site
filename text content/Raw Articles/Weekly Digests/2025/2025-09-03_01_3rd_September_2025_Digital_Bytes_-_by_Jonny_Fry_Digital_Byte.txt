TITLE: A clash over the future of money: central banks vs. stablecoins

central banks, once trusted stewards of stability, face eroded credibility after years of policy missteps - from QE-driven bubbles to misjudged post-pandemic inflation. Meanwhile, stablecoins are emerging as a counterweight, offering instant settlement, programmability and full Treasury backing - often safer than fractional-reserve bank deposits. Whilst the BIS warns stablecoins threaten sovereignty, critics argue they expose flaws in traditional money and, as adoption grows, central banks risk shifting from issuers to regulators. So can they adapt blockchain tools for real-time policy, or cede power to stablecoin ecosystems?
