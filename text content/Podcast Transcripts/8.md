## Key Topics

- Money as **technology plus community**: its form is whatever a community agrees it to be. [file:70]  
- CBDCs’ only truly distinctive feature may be **programmable money**, not basic digitization. [file:70]  
- US seen as “captured” by **stablecoin interests**, with questions over backing, transparency and overlap with money‑market funds. [file:70]  
- Korean **Hangang** digital‑won project: 100k+ firms, central bank as sole custodian, integrating “stablecoin‑like” tech inside the CBDC itself. [file:70]  
- CBDC benefit quadrant: **wholesale–international** is the most plausible sweet spot; retail/domestic already well‑served. [file:70]  
- Concerns: programmable money reducing liquidity, impact on **fractional‑reserve banking**, and dependence on a US‑dollar CBDC vs non‑dollar alternatives. [file:70]  
- US regulatory “maelstrom” (multi‑layer regulators, Section 899 scare) driving other central banks to consider **non‑dollar settlement systems**. [file:70]
